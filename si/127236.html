Driver air bag assembly scrapping (All models)

1. Undeployed driver airbag assembly safety information

Caution

  • Take extra care when handling and storing an undeployed airbag.
  • If the airbag should deploy, the airbag or objects on its surface may fly outward due to the sudden gas generation.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the airbag opening faces the opposite direction of your body.
  • This is to minimize the possibility of injury in the event of the airbag deployment.
  • Never carry the airbag assembly by hanging its wiring or connector on your hand.

  1. When transporting an undeployed airbag, face the trim cover outwards.
  2. When placing an undeployed airbag, make sure that the trim cover is faced upwards.

Note

  • Procedure for transporting an undeployed airbag

Caution

  • Service personnel should refer to the latest service information for the proper airbag transportation procedure.

Note

  • Precautions for the disassembly procedure of the airbag assembly

Caution

  • During the course of a vehicle's service life, certain situations may arise in which the disposal of an undeployed airbag is required.
  • The information provided here is intended to be applied in the proper procedure for disposing of an undeployed airbag.
  • An undeployed airbag must not be disposed of through a normal refuse channel.
  • To dispose of the airbag, it must be deployed.
  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure may result in personal injury when the airbag is deployed.
  • The airbag may be deployed either inside or outside the vehicle.
  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure may be regarded as violation of federal, state or local laws.

2. Deployed driver airbag assembly safety information

Caution

  • To handle activated airbag, wear gloves and safety glasses.
  • When handling the airbag, the safety precautions must be observed.
  • A small amount of sodium hydroxide may be contained on the surface of a deployed airbag.
  • This is a by-product of the deployment reaction and may cause irritation if contact is made with skin or eyes.
  • After handling, wash your hands with soap.

3. Driver airbag assembly (deployment outside vehicle) (method of securing to tire) scrapping

Caution

  • For deployment of an undeployed airbag outside the vehicle, the deployment procedure must be strictly followed.
  • Always wear safety glasses until a deployed airbag is scrapped.
  • Before performing the disposal procedure, you should be familiar with the SRS repair practice and proper airbag handling.
  • Perform the work after reading the procedures thoroughly.
  • Use the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1 for this procedure.
  • The procedure must not be performed when the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1 are not available.

Warning

  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure may result in personal injury. 
  • Never connect the SRS deployment harness to any power source before connecting it to the driver airbag.
  • Leave the deployment harness shorted until the airbag is ready to be deployed.
  • The airbag will immediately deploy when a power source is connected to it.

Caution

  • Below is the driver airbag deployment procedure.

1. Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Note

  • Pull out the key.
  • Wear the safety glasses.

2. Disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery.

3. Remove the airbag from vehicle.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the trim cover faces the opposite direction of your body.
  • When putting down an undeployed airbag, make sure to face the trim cover upward.
  • Placing the trim cover upward will provide space to inflate the airbag in case of unexpected airbag deployment.

4. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

Note

  • Inspect the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1 for damage.
  • If a harness is damaged, discard and obtain a new one.


SST: 1-8528-1002-0 - SRS deployment harness


SST: 5-8840-2881-1 - SRS Deployment harness

Note

  • Short the two SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Caution

  • The SRS deployment harness should be kept shorted and should not be connected to a power source until the airbag is ready to be deployed.

Warning

  • Connecting the deployment harness to the power source should always be the last step in the airbag deployment procedure.
  • Failure to follow the procedure may result in personal injury.

Note

  • Connect the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1.

Note

  • Clear obstacles in a space about 2 m (6.6 feet) in diameter on the ground where the airbag is to be deployed.
  • A paved, outdoor location where there is no person crossing is preferred.
  • If an outdoor location is not available, select a garage with sufficient ventilation where no people are passing through.
  • Confirm that there are no unsecured or flammable objects in the deployment area.

5. Prepare the tire.

Note

  • Prepare 3 tires (15 inch or more) without wheels and 2 tires with wheels of the same size.

6. Connect the SRS deployment harness to the airbag.

Caution

  • Confirm that the SRS deployment harness is securely seated into the airbag connector.
  • Leave the deployment harness shorted until the airbag is ready to be deployed.
  • The airbag will immediately deploy when a power source is connected to it.

7. Fasten the airbag to the tire with wheel.

Note

  • Secure the airbag to a tire with a wheel so that the trim cover faces upward.
  • Wind automotive wire harness (with cross-section of 1.25 mm2 or more) or fix it with wire in 2 locations.
  • Do not wind it on the deployment face.

Note

  • Pile up the tires as shown in the illustration.
  • Secure the tires firmly using ropes at 4 locations to prevent them from tumbling down.

  1. Secured tire of airbag
  2. Tire without wheel
  3. Tire with wheel

Note

  • Stretch the SRS deployment harness to its full length from the airbag.
  • Place a power source near the shorted end of the SRS deployment harness.
  • A vehicle battery with a minimum of 12 V and 2 A is recommended for the power source.

Caution

  • Confirm that no people and no unsecured or flammable objects are present in the area around the airbag.
  • Confirm that the airbag is placed with its trim cover facing up.
  • Let all people in the immediate area know that the airbag is to be deployed.
  • The deployment will be accompanied by a very loud noise which may startle the uninformed.

Note

  • Separate the two banana plugs on the SRS deployment harness.

Caution

  • When the airbag deploys, it may jump up about 30 cm (1 ft) vertically. This is because the gas inside the airbag is rapidly inflated.

Warning

  • The airbag will immediately deploy when a power source is connected to it.

8. Connect the SRS deployment harness to power source.

Caution

  • Once more let all people in the immediate area know that the airbag is to be deployed.

Note

  • Connect the SRS deployment harness to the power source to immediately deploy the airbag.

9. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from power source.

Note

  • Short the two SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Note

  • In the event that the airbag is not deployed after this procedure is followed, immediately proceed to step 11.

Caution

  • When handling the deployed airbag, wear a pair of work gloves and safety goggles to protect your hands and eyes from possible irritation and heat.

Warning

  • When handling the deployed airbag, the safety precautions must be observed.
  • The metal part of the airbag is extremely hot after deployment.
  • Do not touch the metal portions on the airbag for about 30 minutes after deployment.
  • Wait for the inflator module to cool.
  • Do not place the deployed airbag close to any flammable objects.
  • Failure to follow the procedure may result in personal injury.
  • If the deployed airbag must be moved before it is cool, be sure to wear gloves and do not touch the metal surface.

10. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the airbag.

Note

  • Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the airbag as soon after deployment as possible to avoid damage to the SRS deployment harness.
  • Inspect the SRS deployment harness for damage. Replace it if necessary.
  • Dispose of the deployed airbag through a normal refuse channel.

  1. Sturdy vinyl bag
  2. Deployed airbag

Note

  • After handling, wash your hands with soap.

Caution

  • Below is the procedure to be taken in case the airbag is not deployed by the normal procedure.

11. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the airbag.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the trim cover faces the opposite direction of your body.

Caution

  • When putting down an undeployed airbag, make sure to face the trim cover upward.

Warning

  • Placing the trim cover upward will provide space to inflate the airbag in case of unexpected airbag deployment.

12. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

4. Driver airbag assembly (deployment inside vehicle) scrapping

Note

  • Deployment inside vehicle is effective when the vehicle is to be destroyed or salvaged for reusable component parts.
  • This includes, but is not limited to, the following situations:
1The vehicle has completed its useful life.
2The vehicle has been damaged beyond repair in a nondeployment type accident.
3The vehicle has been stripped or damaged beyond repair in a theft.
4Collect reusable components.

Warning

  • Never use the SRS components for other vehicles.

Caution

  • For deployment of an undeployed airbag inside the vehicle, the deployment procedure must be strictly followed.
  • Always wear safety glasses until a deployed airbag is scrapped.
  • Before performing the disposal procedure, you should be familiar with the SRS repair practice and proper airbag handling.
  • Perform the work after reading the procedures thoroughly.
  • Use the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1 for this procedure.

Warning

  • Leave the deployment harness shorted until the airbag is ready to be deployed.
  • The airbag will immediately deploy when a power source is connected to it.

Caution

  • Below is the driver airbag deployment procedure.

1. Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Note

  • Pull out the key.
  • Remove all loose objects from the front seats.
  • To reduce the risk of personal injury by the vehicle glass or interior pieces, cover the windshield area and front door window opening completely with drop cloth, blanket or similar items.
  • Wear the safety glasses.

2. Disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery.

3. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

Note

  • Inspect the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1 for damage.
  • If a harness is damaged, discard and obtain a new one.

SST: 5-8840-2881-1 - SRS Deployment harness

Note

  • Connect the SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1.

4. Remove the airbag from the steering wheel.

5. Connect the SRS deployment harness to the airbag harness connector.

Caution

  • The SRS deployment harness should be kept shorted and should not be connected to a power source until the airbag is ready to be deployed.

6. Install the airbag to the steering wheel.

Caution

  • Be careful the SRS deployment harness is not pinched by other components.
  • Confirm that no people and no unfixed or flammable objects are present in the vehicle or the area around the vehicle.

Note

  • Stretch the SRS deployment harness to its full length from the airbag.

Caution

  • Once more let all people in the immediate area know that the airbag is to be deployed.

Note

  • Connect the SRS deployment harness to the power source to immediately deploy the airbag.

8. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from power source.

Note

  • Short the two SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Note
