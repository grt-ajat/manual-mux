DTC P0711 (Flash code 18) Transmission Fluid Temperature Sensor 1 Circuit Performance (TB-50LS)

1. DTC P0711 description of DTC

Transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 is installed on the valve body assembly together with the harness inside the transmission and detects the ATF temperature inside the valve body. Transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 is a variable resistor and is equipped with a signal circuit and a low reference circuit.
The TCM supplies 5 V to the signal circuit of transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 and grounds the sensor to the low reference circuit. When the ATF temperature inside the valve body is low, the resistance value of the sensor is large, and as the ATF temperature inside the valve body rises, the resistance value of the sensor decreases. When the resistance value of the sensor is large, the TCM detects a high voltage in the transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 signal circuit, and when the resistance value of the sensor is small, it detects a low voltage in the transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 signal circuit. The TCM sets a DTC if changes in the ATF temperature inside the valve body are too small.

2. DTC P0711 condition for setting the DTC

DTCs P0707, P0708、P0712, P0713, P0882, P0883, P0961, P0962, P0963, P0965, P0966, P0967, P0973, P0974, P0976, P0977, P0985, P0986, P1790, P1791, P2719, P2720, P2721, U0073 and U0100 are not set.
The battery voltage is between 10.2 - 15.5 V.
The engine speed is 550 rpm or more.
The ATF temperature detected on the transmission fluid temperature 1 immediately after the ignition switch is turned ON is 20˚ C (68˚ F) or less.
Driving for 10 minutes or more at a 40 km/h (25 mph) or higher vehicle speed detected by the output speed sensor.
The TCM detects that the ATF temperature detected by the transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 does not change for a specified time.

3. DTC P0711 action taken when the DTC sets

Note

  • Euro4 model

When the diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light on the second consecutive driving cycle.
When the diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM requests to illuminate the MIL on the second consecutive driving cycle.
When the diagnostic reports a failure on the second consecutive driving cycle, the TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The ECM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The ECM stores this information in the Freeze Frame/Failure Records.
The ECM stores DTC P0700 in ECM history during the second consecutive driving cycle in which the diagnostic fails.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the self-learning control.
The TCM inhibits the squat control.
The TCM inhibits shifting to the 5th gear.
The TCM fixes the temperature of transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 to 80˚ C.

Note

  • Euro2 and Euro3 model

The TCM flashes the check trans warning light on the second consecutive driving cycle that the diagnostic runs and fails.
If the diagnostic reports a failure on the second consecutive driving cycle, the TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the self-learning control.
The TCM inhibits the squat control.
The TCM inhibits shifting to the 5th gear.
The TCM fixes the temperature of transmission fluid temperature sensor 1 to 80˚ C.
