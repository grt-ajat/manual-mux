Maintenance precautions of security and locks control (Passive Entry and Start System)

1. Maintenance precautions of security and locks control

Warning

  • Individuals with implanted cardiac pacemakers or implanted defibrillators should keep a distance of at least 20 cm from the external antenna, center in-vehicle antenna, rear in-vehicle antenna, and engine start/stop button (immobilizer coil).
  • The radio waves emitted from each antenna of the Passive Entry and Start System (PESS) may influence the operation of other devices.

Caution

  • The electronic key is comprised of precise electronic components. Failure of electronic components will influence keyless entry functions, PESS functions and immobilizer functions. Be aware of the following points:

Do not leave the electronic key in the vehicle.
Do not leave it in a location where it may be exposed to direct sunlight, high temperatures, or high humidity.
Do not drop nor place heavy objects on it.
Do not wet it, nor clean it with ultrasonic cleaning devices.
Do not disassemble it.
Keep it away from flame.
Do not let it touch or be covered by metal objects.
Do not place it near items that give off static, nor electronic medical devices.
Do not carelessly disassemble it except to change the battery.

Caution

  • Before performing repairs to the Passive Entry and Start System (PESS), it is necessary to be aware of the following points.

Unlocking of the doors may be delayed or may not be possible if gloves are worn when using the outside handle to perform unlocking operations.
Unlocking of the doors may be delayed or may not be possible if ice, snow, or dirt is adhered to the outside handle.
The door may not open immediately after the outside handle is gripped.
When the electronic key is within receiving range of the PESS receiver, the doors may unlock due to large amounts of water flowing over the outside handle (in strong rain, car washes, etc.).

Unlocking is not possible for approximately 2 seconds even if the outside handle is touched or the tail gate lock button is pressed.

Even when the electronic key is within the operating range of PESS functions (approximately 80 cm of the outside handle or tail gate), locking/unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the key is too high in the air (or too low to the ground, etc.).
Locking/unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the electronic key is too close to the doors or windows.

If the electronic key is left in the instrument panel or glove box, the warning buzzer may sound due to radio wave conditions.

If the electronic key is outside the vehicle and the power mode is turned OFF, switching to other modes is not possible. (Switching to other modes is possible if the electronic key is returned to within the PESS receiver range.)
