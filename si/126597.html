Defect of turbocharger (4JK1)

1. Defect of turbocharger diagnostics

Note

  • When the engine output has dropped although the engine appears to be normal

Deformed or damaged turbine fins and compressor fins.
Replace.

The fins and housing are touching to each other.
Replace.

There is a large amount of carbon deposits adhering to the turbine exhaust outlet and are in contact with the turbine fins.
Clean or replace.

The turbine shaft catches or rotation is heavy
Replace

The clearance in the axis direction or rotation direction of the turbine shaft is large.
Measure the clearance and replace it if it exceeds the standard value.

Note

  • When the engine output has dropped although the turbocharger appears to be basically normal

Air leakage from the rubber hose of the intake air pipe.
Repair.

Air leakage from the intake manifold.
Repair.

Clogging of the air cleaner element.
Clean or replace.

Sticking of the intake throttle valve.
Repair or replace.

Gas leak from each exhaust section.
Repair or replace.

Excessive exhaust resistance caused by deformed exhaust muffler or carbon clogging.
Repair or replace.

Air leakage from the compressor outlet side.
Repair.

Clogging in the intercooler cooling section.
Clean.

Defective boost sensor.
Replace.

Cause due to the vehicle side, such as dragging of the brake.
Repair.

Note

  • When there are abnormal vibrations in the turbocharger.

Bearing damage or contact of rotating sections with their periphery is the cause.
Oil deterioration, intrusion of foreign materials into the oil pass, clogging of the oil filter, or repeated sudden acceleration or repeated stopping are causes of bearing damage.
Replace

When there is no problem in the lubricating oil system, but there are damages from contacts found in the rotating section and the surrounding sections, it is suspected that the rotating section has become unbalanced or the shaft has been bent. This is caused by bearing worn, or damage on the turbine wheel or compressor wheel made by flying debris.
Replace.

Note

  • Oil is leaking into the exhaust or intake pipe, and the color of exhaust becomes white.

Oil leakage from the turbocharger oil seal.
Repair or replace.

Insufficient oil level due to dirty or clogged oil separator.
Clean or replace.

Clogging in the oil passage of the center housing.
Replace.

Clogging, collapsing or deformation of the oil return pipe.
Repair or replace.

Large turbine shaft clearance
Measure the clearance and replace the turbocharger if it exceeds the specified value

Degraded engine oil.
Replace.

Note

  • When noise is generated from the turbocharger.

Air leakage from the coupling of the rubber hose on the compressor outlet side or gas leakage from the exhaust system.
Repair.

Contact of the turbine fins with the housing.
Replace.

Internal damage due to foreign material from the turbocharger upstream getting caught
Replace

Worn or seizure of the turbine shaft bearing.
Replace.

Note

  • When excessive worn exists in the rotating section.

Degraded engine oil.
Replace.

Clogging in the turbocharger oil feed pipe.
Repair.

Low engine oil pressure.
Repair.
