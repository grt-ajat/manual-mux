Doors do not unlock even when gripping the driver side door handle (Passive Entry and Start System)

1. Doors do not unlock even when gripping the driver side door handle diagnostics

Caution

  • Unlocking of the doors is not possible if the power mode is not OFF.
  • Unlocking of the doors may be delayed or may not be possible if gloves are worn when using the outside door handle to perform unlocking operations.
  • Unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the door is opened immediately after gripping the outside door handle.
  • Unlocking of the doors is not possible when a door is open or ajar even if gripping the door handle.
  • Even when the electronic key is within the operating range of PESS functions (approximately 80 cm of the outside door handle), unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the key is too high in the air (or too low to the ground, etc.).
  • Unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the electronic key is too close to the doors or windows.
  • Unlocking of the doors may not be possible if there is a facility nearby that is emitting strong radio waves.
  • Unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the electronic key is carried together with communication devices such as mobile phones or other wireless communication devices such as laptop computers.
  • Unlocking of the doors may not be possible if the electronic key is touching or covered by other metallic objects, etc.
  • If there is a malfunction in the circuit between the PESS control unit and BCM, "CHECK SYSTEM" is displayed in the multi-information display.

Note

  • Preliminary confirmation and visual inspection

Check the battery voltage.
Charge or replace the battery if battery voltage is low.

Check the fuses.
If an abnormal condition such as a blowout is found in a fuse, inspect or repair the cause of the abnormal condition before replacing the fuse.

Check the grounding point.
Repair or clean any grounding points that are improperly installed or significantly dirty.

Check the BCM.
Observe the DTC with a scan tool and diagnose the applicable DTC.

Check the harness and connector.
Inspect for poor connections at the connectors, worn or bent harnesses, or split wires inside the harness shorted to other circuits.

Check for any interference from aftermarket electronic components such as two-way radios or lights, etc.
Turn OFF or remove any aftermarket electronic components to ensure that there is no influence on inspections or operations.

Perform diagnostic system check-body controls.

Note

  • Components inspection

Observe the PESS registration information with a scan tool.

Check the PESS control unit.
Observe the DTC with a scan tool and diagnose the applicable DTC.

Check the driver side outside handle.
Replace the outside handle if the unlock request sensor is defective.

Check the center in-vehicle antenna.
If the center in-vehicle antenna is defective, replace it.

Check the rear RH in-vehicle antenna.
If the rear RH in-vehicle antenna is defective, replace it.

Check the rear LH in-vehicle antenna.
If the rear LH in-vehicle antenna is defective, replace it.

Check the electronic key battery.
Replace the electronic key battery if it is deteriorated.

Check the electronic key.
If the electronic key is defective, replace it.

Note

  • Circuits inspection

Verify that there are no open circuits, short circuits (to the power supply or ground), or high resistance in the following circuits. When there are abnormalities, repair the applicable circuit, and replace as necessary.
Between the PESS control unit (CN2-35 pin) and the driver side outside handle
Between the driver side outside handle and the battery power supply
Between the driver side outside handle and ground
Between the PESS control unit (CN2-32, 33 pins) and the BCM
Between the PESS control unit (CN2-8, 9, 12, 13, 17 pins) and the PESS receiver

Note

  • If the power mode can be turned from OFF to ON using the engine start/stop button, the electronic key and PESS receiver are normal.
