DTC P1093 (Flash Code 227) Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Too Low (4JK1)

1. DTC P1093 diagnostics

Note

  • An intermittently sticking suction control valve may have allowed the fuel pressure to become low enough to set this DTC.
  • When the engine is running at idle in neutral, the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) should always be within 27 to 33 MPa {3900 to 4800 psi} after warm up.
  • This DTC most likely indicates a loss of fuel pressure due to a restricted suction side fuel line. Inspect for a fuel restriction at the suction side between the fuel supply pump and the fuel tank.
  • If the fuel tank is empty or near empty, air might be allowed to enter the fuel system. With air in the fuel system, the smooth flow of fuel into the supply pump is interrupted and this DTC may be set. Perform air bleeding of the fuel system after refilling.

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JK1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Prioritized DTC

If DTCs P0087, P0091, P0092, P0192, P0193, P0201 - P0204, P2146, or P2149 are set, go to the applicable DTC diagnosis.

3. Circuit diagnosis

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Wait for 30 seconds to reduce the fuel pressure from the fuel rail.

Observe the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Sensor display on the scan tool.

Standard： 0.9 to 1.0 V

If the reading is within the specified range, go to Inspection of fuel pipe.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of fuel rail pressure sensor circuit.

4. Inspection of fuel pipe

Inspect for improper tightening in the fuel system between the fuel tank and the fuel supply pump, for cuts and cracks in the fuel hoses, and the clamps for damage, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • If the fuel hose is not connected securely, air can enter. If the engine speed or the engine load increases while the air has entered the fuel system, fluctuation in the fuel rail pressure occurs, and DTC P1093 may be set.

Inspect for a fuel leak in the high pressure side fuel system between the fuel supply pump and the fuel rail, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • The fuel may leak under the cylinder head cover from the inlet of the high pressure hose. If the fuel leaks under the cylinder head cover, the engine oil level increases. Inspect for a fuel leak into the engine oil.

If the fuel pipe is normal, go to Inspection 1 of suction side fuel line.

5. Inspection 1 of suction side fuel line

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the fuel feed hose from the fuel supply pump.

Install the vacuum pump to the fuel feed hose.

Caution

  • Because the fuel supply pump may be damaged due to foreign material in the vacuum pump, the vacuum pump must be cleaned before connecting to the fuel line.


SST: 5-8840-0279-0 - vacuum pump

Operate the vacuum pump to verify that the fuel flows smoothly.

Caution

  • Note the fuel should not enter the vacuum pump.

Note

  • If there is leakage or restriction in the suction side, which is most likely caused by fuel leakage, a clogged fuel filter, or a kinked or crushed fuel hose or pipe, the fuel from the hose will not flow sufficiently. Foreign material in the fuel tank may also enter the fuel line pickup.

If the fuel does not flow smoothly, repair the fuel line between the fuel tank and the fuel supply pump. Go to Repair verification.

If the fuel flows smoothly, go to Inspection 2 of suction side fuel line.

6. Inspection 2 of suction side fuel line

Replace the fuel hose between the fuel supply pump and the fuel filter with a clear hose.

Bleed the air from the fuel system.

Start the engine.

Allow the engine to idle for at least one minute.

Observe the fuel in the clear hose for one minute with the engine speed at 3000 rpm or higher.

If air bubbles are present, repair the fuel system between the fuel tank and the fuel supply pump. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • Inspect for improper tightening in the fuel system between the fuel tank and the fuel supply pump, for cuts and cracks in the fuel hoses, and the clamps for damage, and repair as necessary.

If air bubbles are not present, go to Inspection of fuel supply pump.

7. Inspection of fuel supply pump

Connect the fuel feed hose to the fuel supply pump.

Disconnect all the injector harness connectors in order to disable the fuel injection.

Disconnect the fuel feed hose from the fuel tank.

Caution

  • Use a pan to collect the fuel from the removed fuel line.

Install the vacuum pump to the fuel feed hose. Verify that the vacuum pump and the fuel line are securely connected.


SST: 5-8840-0279-0 - vacuum pump

Caution

  • The vacuum pump must be cleaned before connecting to the fuel line.

Crank the engine 3 times while monitoring the gauge on the vacuum pump.

Caution

  • Cranking should be performed within 5 seconds at a time, and should not exceed 15 seconds in total.
  • If vacuum may exceed the specified value during the test, release the clamp or open the plug.

Standard： -27 kPa { -0.28 kgf/cm2 / 8 inHg }

If the negative pressure of the specified value is drawn, go to Inspection of injector.

If the negative pressure of the specified value is not drawn, go to Inspection of suction control valve circuit.

8. Inspection of injector

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

If the fuel pipe is removed, reconnect it.

Start the engine and continue to run the engine for at least one minute.

Perform the Cylinder Balance Test with a scan tool.

Command each injector OFF and verify the engine speed changes for each injector.

If the engine speed changes for all the injectors, go to Pressure limiter valve replacement.

If the engine speed does not change, go to the applicable cylinder injector replacement.

9. Inspection of fuel rail pressure sensor circuit

Inspect the connection status of the fuel rail pressure sensor harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the fuel rail pressure sensor circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for high resistance in the fuel rail pressure sensor circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, replace the fuel rail pressure sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) FRP sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) FRP sensor installation".

10. Inspection of suction control valve circuit

Inspect the connection status of the suction control valve harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the suction control valve circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for high resistance in the suction control valve circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Fuel supply pump replacement.

11. Pressure limiter valve replacement

Replace the pressure limiter valve. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • There is a possibility that the pressure limiter valve may be stuck open or the valve opening pressure may have fallen down.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) pressure limiter removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) pressure limiter installation".

12. Injector replacement

Replace the injector. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • When the injector has been replace, set the injector ID code on the ECM.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) injector removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) injector installation".

13. Fuel supply pump replacement

Replace the fuel supply pump. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • If the fuel supply pump is replaced, the fuel filter element is also required to be replaced at the same time.
  • When the fuel supply pump is replaced, perform the learning of the fuel supply pumps for the ECM.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) fuel supply pump removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) fuel supply pump installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) fuel filter element removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JK1) fuel filter element installation".

2. DTC P1093 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Operate the vehicle within the conditions for setting the DTC or within the parameters observed in the Freeze Frame.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
