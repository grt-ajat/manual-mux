DTC P0882 (Flash code 72) TCM Power Input Signal Low (TB-50LS)

1. DTC P0882 description of DTC

The TCM monitors whether the system voltage is within the normal range on the ignition power supply circuits. The TCM sets a DTC when it detects that the ignition voltage is low. Also, when there is a malfunction in the charging system, the battery charge warning light illuminates.

2. DTC P0882 condition for setting the DTC

The ignition switch is on.
All of the following conditions are satisfied.
DTCs U0073 and U0100 are not set.
The engine speed is 1,000 rpm or more.
The engine speed sensor is normal.
Any of the following conditions is satisfied.
DTC P0717 is not set.
The turbine rotation is 1,000 rpm.
The TCM detects that the ignition voltage is 9 V or less for 20 seconds.

3. DTC P0882 action taken when the DTC sets

Note

  • Euro4 model

When a diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light.
The TCM requests the MIL to be illuminated when the diagnostic runs and fails.
The TCM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The ECM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The ECM stores this information in the Freeze Frame/Failure Records.
The ECM stores DTC P0700 in ECM history at the time the diagnostic fails.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the lock-up timing control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the engine torque control.
The TCM switches the mode to the emergency mode.

Note

  • Euro2 and Euro3 model

When a diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light.
The TCM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnosis fails. The TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the lock-up timing control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the engine torque control.
The TCM switches the mode to the emergency mode.
