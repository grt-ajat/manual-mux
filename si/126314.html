DTC P0335 (Flash Code 15) Crankshaft Position Sensor Circuit (4JJ1)

1. DTC P0335 diagnostics

Note

  • If there is electromagnetic interference in the CKP sensor circuits, this DTC may set.
  • If there is an intermittent CKP sensor signal pulse, this DTC may set.

Caution

  • If the CKP sensor signal pulse is lost while the engine is running, the engine will stop.

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Prioritized DTC

Start the engine. Note a slight start delay may be noticed.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.

If DTCs P0340 or P0697 are set at the same time, go to the applicable DTC diagnosis.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the CKP sensor harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Measure the voltage between the CKP sensor 5 V reference circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in 5 V reference circuit.

4. Circuit diagnosis 2

Measure the voltage between the CKP sensor signal circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 4.7 to 5.3 V

If the reading is within the specified range, go to Circuit diagnosis 3.

If the reading is more than the specified value, go to Inspection 1 for short circuit in signal circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit and short circuit in signal circuit.

5. Circuit diagnosis 3

Connect a test lamp between the CKP sensor signal circuit and the body ground, and measure the voltage between the CKP sensor signal circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection 2 for short circuit in signal circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 4.

6. Circuit diagnosis 4

Measure the voltage between the CKP sensor signal circuit and the low reference circuit.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection of CKP sensor harness connector.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in low reference circuit.

7. Inspection for open circuit in 5 V reference circuit

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the CKP sensor 5 V reference circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • The CKP sensor shares the 5 V reference circuit with other sensors. A fault condition in the 5 V reference circuit may set DTCs on sensors that share this circuit.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of ECM harness connector.

8. Inspection 1 for short circuit in signal circuit

Inspect the CKP sensor signal circuit for a short to the power supply circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • The CKP sensor may be damaged if the sensor signal circuit shorts to the power supply.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

9. Inspection 2 for short circuit in signal circuit

Inspect for a short to the 5 V reference circuit in the CKP sensor signal circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

10. Inspection for open circuit and short circuit in signal circuit

Inspect the CKP sensor signal circuit for an open circuit, high resistance, a short to ground or a short to the low reference circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of ECM harness connector.

11. Inspection for open circuit in low reference circuit

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the CKP sensor low reference circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • The CKP sensor shares the low reference circuit with other sensors. A fault condition in the low reference circuit may set DTCs on sensors that share this circuit.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of ECM harness connector.

12. Inspection of CKP sensor harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the CKP sensor harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to Inspection of CKP sensor.

13. Inspection of CKP sensor

Inspect the CKP sensor and the sensor rotor for the following conditions, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Sensor damage
Loose or incorrectly installed sensor
Excessive gap
Foreign material passing between sensor and sensor rotor
Sensor rotor damage
Loose or incorrectly installed sensor rotor

If it is normal, replace the CKP sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) CKP sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) CKP sensor installation".

14. Inspection of ECM harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to ECM replacement.

15. ECM replacement

Replace the ECM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM setting".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM writing".

2. DTC P0335 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Operate the vehicle within the conditions for setting the DTC or within the parameters observed in the Freeze Frame.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
