EGR control system check (4JK1)

1. EGR control system check description of function

The EGR system re-circulates a part of exhaust gas back into the intake manifold, which results in reducing NOx emissions.
Higher operability and emission reduction are realized by controlling the EGR system. A control current from the ECM operates a motor to control the lift amount of the EGR valve. In addition, the micro processor installed in the EGR valve is used for detection of actual valve lift amount and precision control of EGR amount.
The EGR is activated when specified conditions of engine speed, engine water temperature, intake air temperature and barometric pressure are met. Then, the valve opening angle is obtained based on engine speed and the target fuel injection amount. From this valve opening angle, motor drive duty is determined, based on which the valve is driven. When the EGR is activated, the intake throttle valve is closed by adjusting to the appropriate intake manifold negative pressure.

Note

  • Operating conditions for EGR control

Engine water temperature 5 - 100 ℃ (41 - 212 °F)
Intake air temperature 5 ℃ (41 °F) or above
Barometric pressure 90 kPa (13 psi) or above

2. EGR control system check

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JK1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Prioritized DTC.

If DTCs inhibiting EGR control are set, go to the applicable DTC diagnosis.

3. Inspection of EGR control parts

Inspect for the following conditions, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

An EGR valve gasket that is missing or damaged.
Stuck EGR valve.
EGR gas leaks from the EGR passage between the exhaust manifold and the intake manifold.
Collapsing of the EGR passage between the exhaust manifold and the intake manifold or a condition of restricted flow.
Condition of restricted exhaust system.
Clogged air cleaner element, collapsing of the duct between the air cleaner and the intake manifold, or a condition of restricted flow.
Leakage in the air intake system.
Water intrusion into the air intake system.
Contamination or foreign material blocking the inlet of the MAF sensor.
Misdetection or delayed response of the MAF sensor.
Stuck intake throttle valve.

Water temperature sensor malfunction.

Refer to "1.Engine 1D.Cooling(4JK1) engine coolant temperature sensor inspection".

Malfunction of the barometric pressure sensor.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JK1) barometric pressure sensor inspection".

If EGR control parts are normal, go to EGR valve diagnosis.

4. EGR valve diagnosis

Put the transmission in the neutral position to apply the parking brake.

Start the engine.

Use a scan tool while depressing the accelerator pedal to W.O.T. to verify that the difference between the Desired Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Position display and the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Position display is within the specified range.

Specified value： 5 %

If the reading is within the specified range, go to Inspection of EGR valve operation.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of EGR valve.

5. Inspection of EGR valve operation

Perform the EGR solenoid control with a scan tool.

Command the EGR valve INCREASE and DECREASE, and verify that the difference between the Desired Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Position display and the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) Valve Position display is within the specified range.

Specified value： 5 %

If the reading is within the specified range, go to Intake throttle valve diagnosis.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of EGR valve.

6. Intake throttle valve diagnosis

Verify that the difference between the Desired Intake Throttle Valve Position display and the Intake Throttle Valve Position display is within the specified range on the scan tool.

Note

  • If the intake throttle motor is OFF, the Intake Throttle Valve Position display indicates a value more than or equal to 100%.

Specified value： 5 %

If the reading is within the specified range, go to Inspection of intake throttle valve operation.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of intake throttle valve.

7. Inspection of intake throttle valve operation

Perform the Intake Throttle Solenoid Control with a scan tool.

Command the intake throttle valve INCREASE and DECREASE, and verify that the difference between the Desired Intake Throttle Valve Position display and the Intake Throttle Valve Position display is within the specified range.

Specified value： 5 %

If the reading is within the specified range, the system is normal.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of intake throttle valve.

8. Inspection of EGR valve

Inspect the EGR valve for the following conditions, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Clogging of the EGR valve due to foreign material
Excessive deposits
Valve shaft bending

If it is normal, go to Inspection of EGR valve harness connector.

9. Inspection of EGR valve harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the EGR valve harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the EGR valve circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, replace the EGR valve. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1H.Aux. Emission Control Devices(4JK1) EGR valve removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1H.Aux. Emission Control Devices(4JK1) EGR valve installation".

10. Inspection of intake throttle valve

Inspect the intake throttle valve for the following conditions, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Clogging of the intake throttle valve due to foreign material
Excessive deposits
Bent butterfly valve

Caution

  • If any sticking is found, replace the intake throttle valve.

If it is normal, go to Inspection of intake throttle valve circuit.

11. Inspection of intake throttle valve circuit

Inspect the connection status of the intake throttle valve harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the intake throttle valve circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for high resistance in each intake throttle valve circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, replace the intake throttle valve. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JK1) intake throttle valve removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JK1) intake throttle valve installation".

12. Repair verification

Reconnect all of the disconnected parts and harness connectors.

Go to Prioritized DTC.
