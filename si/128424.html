DTC P2774 (Flash code 75) Four Wheel Drive (4WD) Low Signal Low (TB-50LS)

1. DTC P2774 description of DTC

When the 4WD low signal is input from the TCCM via CAN communication, the TCM switches to the 4L mode. Also, the TCM calculates the gear ratio of the transfer based on the vehicle speed signal sent from the ECM via CAN communication. When the TCM detects a disagreement between the gear ratio of the transfer and the 4WD high signal from the TCCM, the DTC is set.

2. DTC P2774 condition for setting the DTC

DTCs P0707, P0708, P0722, P0882, P0883, P0961, P0962, P0963, P0965, P0966, P0967, P0973, P0974, P0976, P0977, P0985, P0986, P1790, P1791, P2719, P2720, P2721, U0073 and U0100 are not set.
The battery voltage is between 10.2 - 15.5 V.
The engine speed is 550 rpm or more.
The vehicle speed is 37.4 km/h (23 mph) or more (4JJ1 engine).
The vehicle speed is 35.8 km/h (22 mph) or more (4JK1 engine).
The vehicle speed sensor signal is normal.
The R or D position switch is ON.
The output shaft speed is 1500 rpm or more.
The TCM detects that the transfer gear ratio calculated at the TCM and the 4WD high signal from the TCCM do not agree for 10 seconds.

3. DTC P2774 action taken when the DTC sets

Note

  • Euro4 model

When a diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light.
The TCM requests the MIL to be illuminated when the diagnostic runs and fails.
The TCM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The ECM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The ECM stores this information in the Freeze Frame/Failure Records.
The ECM stores DTC P0700 in ECM history at the time the diagnostic fails.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the self-learning control.
The TCM inhibits shifting to the 5th gear.

Note

  • Euro2 and Euro3 model

When a diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light.
The TCM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the slip lock-up control.
The TCM inhibits the self-learning control.
The TCM inhibits shifting to the 5th gear.
