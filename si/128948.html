4WD switch system check (All models)

1. 4WD switch system check description of function

The 4WD switch is installed to the center console. The 2H signal circuit turns ON when the 4WD switch is in the 2H position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 2H position. The 4H signal circuit turns ON when the 4WD switch is in the 4H position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 4H position. Both the 2H signal circuit and 4H signal circuit turn ON when the 4WD switch is in the 4L position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 4L position.
The TCCM drives the transfer shift actuator to shift the gear position inside the transfer to the 4WD switch position operated by the driver.

2. 4WD switch system check

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "204.Description Driveline, Axle 17C.Functional Inspection(All models) diagnostic system check-transfer case controls".

2. Checking DTCs

Drive the vehicle in 2H, 4H, and 4L modes respectively.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.

If a DTC has been detected, perform the applicable diagnosis for the DTC.

If no DTC has been detected, go to Circuit diagnosis 1.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the 4WD switch harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Observe the Mode-Switch 2H-port Status display with a scan tool.

If ON is displayed, go to Inspection 1 for short circuit in signal circuit.

If OFF is displayed, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

4. Circuit diagnosis 2

Observe the Mode-Switch 4H-port Status display with a scan tool.

If ON is displayed, go to Inspection 2 for short circuit in signal circuit.

If OFF is displayed, go to Circuit diagnosis 3.

5. Circuit diagnosis 3

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the TCCM harness connector.

Measure the resistance between the 2H signal circuit and 4H signal circuit at the 4WD switch harness connector.

Resistance： 10 MΩ

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection of 4WD switch harness connector.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for short circuit between signal circuits.

6. Inspection 1 for short circuit in signal circuit

Inspect for a short to ground in the 4WD switch 2H signal circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of TCCM harness connector.

7. Inspection 2 for short circuit in signal circuit

Inspect for a short to ground in the 4WD switch 4H signal circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of TCCM harness connector.

8. Inspection for short circuit between signal circuits

Inspect for a short circuit between signal circuits, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of TCCM harness connector.

9. Inspection of 4WD switch harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the 4WD switch harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to Inspection of 4WD switch.

10. Inspection of 4WD switch

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Remove the 4WD switch.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9C.Electrical Control, Entertainment(All models) 4WD switch removal".

Inspect the 4WD switch, and repair or replace as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9C.Electrical Control, Entertainment(All models) 4WD switch inspection".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9C.Electrical Control, Entertainment(All models) 4WD switch installation".

11. Inspection of TCCM harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the TCCM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, replace the TCCM as necessary. Go to Replacement of TCCM.

12. Replacement of TCCM

Replace the TCCM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) TCCM removal".

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) TCCM installation".

13. Repair verification

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Drive the vehicle in 2H, 4H, and 4L modes respectively.

Verify the lighting status of the 4WD indicator light and the 4WD low indicator light.
