DTC P0234 (Flash Code 42) Turbocharger Overboost Condition (4JJ1)

1. DTC P0234 diagnostics

Note

  • Verify that the boost pressure sensor signal is normal with a scan tool. Compare Boost Pressure with Desired Boost Pressure under all load conditions, and observe whether an excessively high value is displayed.
  • Gasoline contamination in the fuel may cause the DTC to be set.

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Prioritized DTC

If DTCs P0045, P0237, P0238, P0638, P2227, P2228, or P2229 are set at the same time, go to the applicable DTC diagnosis.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Observe the difference between the Boost Pressure display and the Barometric Pressure (BARO) display on the scan tool.

Note

  • If the boost pressure on the scan tool shows a value close to the barometric pressure while the ignition switch is ON with the engine stopped, it is considered normal.

Standard： 10 kPa { 1.5 psi }

If the difference is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the difference is less than the specified value, go to Inspection of mechanical parts.

4. Circuit diagnosis 2

Verify that the Barometric Pressure (BARO) display is a value close to the surrounding barometric pressure.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) barometric pressure sensor inspection".

If the display is normal, go to Inspection of boost pressure sensor circuit.

If the display is abnormal, go to Inspection of barometric pressure sensor circuit.

5. Inspection of mechanical parts

Inspect for the following as the cause of high boost pressure, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Incorrect routing of the vacuum hose of the turbocharger nozzle control actuator.
Sticking of the turbocharger nozzle control actuator or the solenoid valve.

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) Turbocharger control system check".

Sticking of the intake throttle valve. Perform the Intake Throttle Solenoid Control with a scan tool.
Boost pressure sensor signal error caused by oil intrusion within the air intake pipe. If the oil is attached to inside the piping, intercooler, or turbocharger, it is necessary to wipe it off.

6. Inspection of boost pressure sensor circuit

Inspect the connection status of the boost pressure sensor harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the boost pressure sensor circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for high resistance in the boost pressure sensor low reference circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, replace the boost pressure sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1F.Induction(4JJ1) boost pressure sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1F.Induction(4JJ1) boost pressure sensor installation".

7. Inspection of barometric pressure sensor circuit

Inspect the connection status of the barometric pressure sensor harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the barometric pressure sensor circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for high resistance in the barometric pressure sensor 5 V reference circuit and the signal circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, replace the barometric pressure sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) barometric pressure sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) barometric pressure sensor installation".

2. DTC P0234 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Start the engine.

Drive the vehicle with at least 50% of the engine load for 10 seconds while comparing the Boost Pressure display with the Desired Boost Pressure display on a scan tool.

Verify that the difference between the Boost Pressure display and the Desired Boost Pressure display is within the specified range.

Standard： 20 kPa { 3 psi }

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Verification of Prioritized DTC
