Rear disc brake reassembly (All models)

1. Brake caliper assembly reassembly

1. Install the piston seal to the brake caliper assembly.

Note

  • Apply AMMIX rubber grease or equivalent to the ring seal, and then install to the cylinder.

Caution

  • Take care not to twist the ring seal.

2. Install the dust boot to the piston.

Note

  • Lightly apply NIGLUBE RX-2 rubber grease or equivalent to the inner and outer lips of the dust boot.

Caution

  • Do not mix-up the top and bottom of the dust boot.

3. Install the piston to the brake caliper assembly.

Note

  • Apply clean brake fluid to the piston, then install to the caliper.
  • Use only finger pressure when inserting the piston into the cylinder.

Caution

  • Do not use tools such as a mallet or impact wrench as they can damage the cylinder wall or ring seal.

Note

  • If the piston cannot be pushed only using finger pressure, place a scrapped brake pad or a piece of wood on top of the piston and use 2 large-sized C-clamps to insert the piston into the cylinder.

  1. Large-sized C-clamp
  2. Brake pad or piece of wood
  3. Caliper

Caution

  • Insert it slowly and evenly.
  • Do not hit the piston with a hammer, or wrench the piston surface with pliers, etc.

Note

  • After inserting the piston into the cylinder, push the circumference of the dust boot towards the cylinder body side surface to assemble.
  • Carefully push the protruding section of the dust boot into the cylinder body groove.

  1. Protruding section of the dust boot
  2. Cylinder body groove

4. Inspect the piston.

Note

  • Confirm that the piston moves smoothly in the cylinder.
  • If the piston does not move or hardly moves, overhauling or replacement of the caliper is required.
  • Confirm that the pleated portion of the dust boot does not protrude from the piston end.
  • Slightly pull out the piston to confirm that the boot is firmly installed to the cylinder body groove.

  1. Piston end
  2. Pleated portion of the dust boot
  3. Piston

5. Install the bleeder screw to the brake caliper assembly.

Tightening torque： 9 to 13 N・m { 0.9 to 1.3 kgf・m / 80 to 115 lb・in }

6. Install the cap to the bleeder screw.
