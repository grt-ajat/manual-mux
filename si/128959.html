DTC C0306 Motor A or B Circuit (All models)

1. DTC C0306 description of DTC

The transfer shift actuator is installed in the transfer assembly. Changeovers between the 2-wheel and 4-wheel drives and between high and low are conducted by the single actuator. The actuator is connected with the shift drum. By changing the direction of the current flowing in the actuator drive circuit, the shift drum connected with the actuator rotates clockwise or counterclockwise. According to the shift drum rotation, each shift rod shifts so that the gear position inside the transfer is shifted to the 4WD switch position operated by the driver. The transfer shift actuator has the built-in limit switches 1 to 4. The 2 - 4 switch and the neutral switch are installed in the transfer. The TCCM recognizes the current transfer drive status from a combination of the limit switch 1 to 4, the 2 - 4 switch, and neutral switch.
The TCCM sets this DTC when it detects that either one of the transfer shift actuator drive 1 circuit or transfer shift actuator drive 2 circuit is open-circuited or short-circuited to the ground.

2. DTC C0306 condition for setting the DTC

The battery voltage is 10 - 16 V.
When a status that either one of the transfer shift actuator drive 1 circuit or transfer shift actuator drive 2 circuit is open-circuited or short-circuited to the ground is detected.

3. DTC C0306 action taken when the DTC sets

The TCCM turns on the check 4WD warning light when a failure is detected by performing a diagnosis.
Maintain the current transfer position. Maintain the 4WD switch position recognized previously.
The TCCM is reset after the ignition switch is turned OFF.
