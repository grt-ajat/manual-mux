DTC B2725 Mode Switch Circuit (All models)

1. DTC B2725 description of DTC

The 4WD switch is installed to the center console. The 2H signal circuit turns ON when the 4WD switch is in the 2H position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 2H position. The 4H signal circuit turns ON when the 4WD switch is in the 4H position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 4H position. Both the 2H signal circuit and 4H signal circuit turn ON when the 4WD switch is in the 4L position, and the TCCM recognizes that the 4WD switch is in the 4L position.
The TCCM drives the transfer shift actuator to shift the gear position inside the transfer to the 4WD switch position operated by the driver.
If the TCCM detects that both the 2H signal circuit and the 4H signal circuit are OFF, this DTC is set.

2. DTC B2725 condition for setting the DTC

The battery voltage is 10 - 16 V.
When OFF status has been detected on both the 2H signal circuit and 4H signal circuit.

3. DTC B2725 action taken when the DTC sets

The TCCM illuminates the check 4WD warning light when the diagnostic runs and fails.
Maintain the transfer position. (The maintained position will vary due to the section of the open circuit.)
The TCCM is reset after the ignition switch is turned OFF.
