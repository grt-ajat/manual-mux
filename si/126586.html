Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess (4JK1)

1. Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess description of symptom

The engine output is less than expected. The engine rpm hardly increases or does not increase at all even when the accelerator pedal is depressed.

2. Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess diagnostics

Note

  • Preliminary inspection

Perform the inspection for the engine control system.
Compare the vehicle with a similar vehicle. Confirm if any problem exists in the vehicle.
Remove the air cleaner and inspect for dirt, collapsing of the air duct or any condition that restricts the flow.
Confirm if the tire size has been changed.
Confirm if excessively heavy loads are being carried.
Inspect for clutch slip.
Inspect for brake drag.
Inspect whether the transmission shift pattern and down shift operation are normal.
Inspect the fuel quality.
Inspect the engine oil level and quality.
Use a scan tool in order to confirm that the Vehicle Speed display matches with the value of the speedometer on the vehicle.
Confirm if the ground of the ECM is free of dirt, and is firmly secured in the correct position.
Check the injector ID code of each cylinder.
Check the scan tool data list.
Check the Service Bulletin.

Note

  • Sensor inspection

Compare each display of the Engine Coolant Temperature (ECT), Intake Air Temperature (IAT) and Fuel Temperature to the scan tool data list with the engine stopped and cold. If the difference between temperature values is 5˚ C (9˚ F) or above, inspect for high resistance in each circuit or sensor failure with the engine is cold.
{When the ignition switch is turned ON, the MAF sensor is heated, and as a result, the IAT shows a higher value than the normal intake air temperature.}
Check the Mass Air Flow (MAF) sensor display with a scan tool.
Check the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) sensor display on the scan tool. It is normal if the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Sensor display shows 0.9 - 1.0 V when the ignition switch is turned ON without starting the engine after waiting at least 30 seconds after the stop of engine operation. If not, inspect for high resistance in each circuit and any sensor failure.
Fully depress the accelerator pedal while idling in neutral, and confirm the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) and Desired Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) displays. It is normal if the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display follows the Desired Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display within the tolerance of 5 MPa {725 psi}.
Verify the accelerator pedal position. It is normal if the Accelerator Pedal Position (APP) display changes linearly from 0 to 100 % in conformity to the accelerator pedal operation.
Observe the Barometric Pressure (BARO) display. The Barometric Pressure (BARO) display should indicate near surrounding barometric pressure. (Standard output engine)
Observe the Boost Pressure and Barometric Pressure (BARO) displays with the ignition switch ON and the engine OFF. It is normal if the difference between both data displays is within 7.0 kPa (1.0 psi). (High-output engine)

Note

  • Fuel system inspection

Inspect for air intrusion in the fuel system.
Inspect for water intrusion in the fuel.
Inspect for external fuel leakage or excessive engine oil.
Inspect whether the fuel is becoming waxy or freezing.
Inspect whether the fuel filter indicator light turns ON continuously or frequently when the engine is driven.
Inspect the fuel filter element for clogging.
Inspect for fuel leakage from the pressure limiter and injector.
Inspect the injector nozzle for sticking.
Inspect for collapsing, twisting, improper tightening, cracking, and clogging in the fuel system between the fuel tank and fuel supply pump.
Inspect the vent valve and hose of the fuel tank for clogging.
{The piping may be clogged due to foreign matters from the fuel tank mixed into the fuel system.}
Inspect the fuel supply pump operation.
{The fuel supply pump must be timed with the engine, and the adjusted value should be written into the ECM.}
Conduct the cylinder balance test with a scan tool.
Perform the injector forced drive with a scan tool.
Confirm the Fuel Compensation display of each cylinder with a scan tool while idling.

Note

  • Air intake system inspection

Inspect the air cleaner and air intake duct for clogging, holes and leakage.
Inspect for any condition that restricts the flow within the turbocharger inlet duct.
Inspect the intake throttle valve to see if it is stuck closed.
Inspect for clogging or air leakage in the intake manifold.
Inspect the MAF sensor for dirt and damage.
Perform the swirl control solenoid control with a scan tool. Inspect the diaphragm valve operation when the "ON/OFF" command is executed on the diaphragm valve.
Inspect the turbocharger turbine wheel, shaft or compressor wheel for worn and damage.
Inspect the operation of wastegate valve of the turbocharger. (Standard output engine)
Inspect the operation of nozzle control actuator of the turbocharger. Refer to Turbocharger control system check. (High-output engine)

Note

  • Exhaust system inspection

Inspect for any condition that restricts the flow of the exhaust system.

Note

  • Engine mechanical inspection

Inspect the compression pressure of the engine.
Inspect the valve clearance.
Inspect the valve spring for damage and fatigue.
Inspect the camshaft for worn.

Note

  • Other inspections

Inspect whether the EGR system is operating properly. Refer to EGR control system check.
Confirm the Neutral switch display on a scan tool.
Inspect the engine for an overheating condition.
Inspect the operation of the A/C.

Note

  • Other than automatic transmission model

Inspect whether the EGR system is operating properly. Refer to EGR control system check.
Confirm the Neutral switch display on a scan tool.
Inspect the engine for an overheating condition.
Inspect the operation of the A/C.
Inspect the operation of the torque converter clutch.
