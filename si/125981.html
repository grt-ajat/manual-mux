Radiator inspection (4JJ1)

1. Radiator inspection

1. Inspect the radiator.

Note

  • A bent fin causes ventilation to fall, and may cause overheat.
  • Any bent fin must be straightened.
  • When straightening, pay attention especially to the bottom of the fin.
  • Remove filth, insects, or any other foreign matter.

2. Clean the radiator using detergent.

Note

  • Wash the inside of the radiator and the engine coolant path with water and a mild detergent.
  • Remove every sign of scales and rust.

3. Inspect the radiator using the radiator cap tester.

Note

  • Send the air of the specified pressure from the filler neck to the radiator using a radiator cap tester.
  • Leakage from the radiator
  • Leakage from the coolant pump
  • Leakage from the water hose.
  • Check the bulge of the rubber hose.

Note

  • Clean the radiator core if it is found dirty.

2. Radiator cleaning

1. Clean the radiator using tap water.

Note

  • Cleaning of inside of radiator and water passage
  • Wash the inside of the radiator and coolant path with water.
  • Remove all scales and rust.
  • Washing the radiator core

Caution

  • Do not apply high-pressure washing to the radiator, intercooler, and peripheral parts while they are installed in the vehicle.

Note

  • Seal the hose connection with a cap or fabric tape.
  • Wet the front and rear surfaces of the radiator sufficiently.
  • For fouling that can be removed by splashing tap water, thoroughly wash them off.
  • Do not use a high-pressure washing machine or brush.

2. Apply detergent to the radiator.

Note

  • Apply a type of mild detergent which contains approximately 8 % of surface acting agent, and leave it for 10 minutes.
  • Use of material other than mild detergent may cause corrosion.
  • Spray mild detergent on the front and rear surfaces of the fin, and leave it for 10 minutes.

3. Clean the radiator using the high-pressure washing machine.

Note

  • Wash off mild detergent with a high-pressure washing machine.
  • Wash the radiator from the engine side.
  • Apply high-pressure water perpendicular to the fins.
  • Do not bring the high-pressure water too close to the fins.

Caution

  • Watch for deformation of the fins.

  1. Radiator

Note

  • After washing, inspect the fins to confirm that dirt has been completely washed off.
  • If washing is insufficient, wash them again.
  • If dirt remains, fine black particles are seen attached on the fins.

4. Clean the radiator using tap water.

Note

  • Wash off detergent ingredients on the fins and resin parts thoroughly with the tap water.
  • Remove the seal on the hose connection.
  • Thoroughly clean and dry the hose connection.
