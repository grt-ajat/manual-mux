Starting system check (4JJ1)

1. Starting system check description of function

This diagnostic table is an organized approach to identifying a condition that causes an engine not to start. The appropriate system diagnostic can be performed using the diagnostic table. The diagnostic table assumes the following conditions are met.

The battery is fully charged and terminals are cleaned and tight.
The engine cranking speed is normal.
There is adequate fuel in the fuel tank.
There is no air in the fuel line.
The fuel filter is clean.
The fuse and slow blow fuse are normal.

2. Starting system check

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. DTC verification

Install a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for 30 seconds.

Crank the engine for a total of 15 seconds.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.

If a DTC is set, go to the applicable DTC diagnosis.

If a DTC is not set, go to Circuit diagnosis 1.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Connect the ECM harness connector.

While listening to or feeling the operation sounds of the ECM main relay, turn the ignition switch ON and OFF. Wait for 7 seconds during the transition.

If you can hear an operating sound, go to Scan tool data verification 1.

If you cannot hear an operating sound, go to Inspection of battery power supply circuit.

4. Inspection of battery power supply circuit

Replace the ECM main relay with a heater relay or a known good relay, and start the engine. If the engine starts, replace the ECM main relay. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for poor connections of the ECM main relay, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Use a test lamp to inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the ECM main relay power supply circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the ECM main relay ground circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the battery power supply circuit between the ECM and the ECM main relay, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for corrosion or tightness of the ECM main relay ground terminal and the ECM ground terminal, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

5. Scan tool data verification 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch for 30 seconds to lower the fuel pressure inside the fuel rail.

Turn ON the ignition switch. Do not start the engine.

Observe the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display on the scan tool.

Standard： 0 MPa { 0 psi }

If the reading is equal to the specified value, go to Scan tool data verification 2.

If the reading is outside the specified range, go to Inspection of harness connector.

6. Scan tool data verification 2

Caution

  • When the vehicle has run out of fuel, air may get into the fuel system.

Verify that the fuel tank has enough fuel and the quality of fuel is good.

While cranking the engine for 5 seconds, observe the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display on the scan tool.

Standard： 20 MPa { 2900 psi }

If the reading is less than or equal to the specified value, go to Scan tool data verification 3.

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection of sensor.

7. Scan tool data verification 3

While cranking the engine for 5 seconds, observe the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Regulator Valve Feedback Current display on the scan tool.

Standard： 1500 mA

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, refer to Fuel system check, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the reading is less than the specified value, repair a short to ground in the suction control valve low side circuit. Go to Repair verification.

8. Inspection of harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the suction control valve harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect the connection status of the suction control valve circuit at the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, replace the suction control valve. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • When the suction control valve is replaced, perform the learning of the fuel supply pumps on the ECM.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JJ1) FRP regulator removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JJ1) FRP regulator installation".

9. Inspection of sensor

Refer to the related DTC diagnosis to inspect the following sensors and circuits, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Engine coolant temperature sensor
Barometric pressure sensor
Boost pressure sensor
Intake throttle position sensor

If the sensor is normal, go to Inspection of engine mechanical parts.

10. Inspection of engine mechanical parts

Inspect for the following conditions, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Improper engine mechanical timing
Heavily restricted intake system or clogged exhaust system
Poor engine compression
Water or gasoline contamination in fuel

If engine mechanical parts are normal, go to Repair verification.

11. ECM replacement

Replace the ECM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM setting".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM writing".

12. Repair verification

Reconnect all of the disconnected parts and harness connectors.

Start the engine.

If the engine starts, go to Diagnostic system check.

If the engine does not start, go to DTC verification.
