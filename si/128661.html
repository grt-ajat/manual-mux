Lost communication with the ICU (Immobilizer)

1. Lost communication with the ICU description of function

The ECM, TCM, TCCM, EHCU, ICU, SRS control unit, BCM, and instrument panel cluster communicate with a scan tool via the CAN communication bus. The EGR valve, DRM, and auto A/C control unit use the same CAN communication bus when the vehicle is running, but do not use it for communicating with a scan tool.

2. Lost communication with the ICU

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "229.Description Security and Locks Control 42C.Functional Inspection(Immobilizer) diagnostic system check-immobilizer controls".

2. ICU harness connector inspection

Inspect the connection status of the ICU harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to Circuit diagnosis 1.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Inspect the METER (+B) 10 A fuse, and repair as necessary. If the fuse blows out again, repair the short to ground in the circuit relating to the METER (+B) 10 A fuse. Go to Repair verification.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the ICU harness connector.

Connect a test lamp between the battery power supply circuit and the body ground.

If the test lamp illuminates, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the test lamp does not illuminate, repair the open circuit or high resistance in the battery power supply circuit. Go to Repair verification.

4. Circuit diagnosis 2

If it is connected, disconnect the scan tool from the DLC.

Measure the resistance between the CAN high and CAN low side circuits of the DLC.

Standard： 50 to 70 Ω

Note

  • The combined resistance of 120Ω resistance inside the ECM and 120Ω resistance inside the instrument panel cluster is 60Ω.

If the resistance is not within the specified range, go to CAN serial data circuit inspection.

If the resistance is within the specified range, go to ICU ground circuit inspection.

5. ICU ground circuit inspection

Inspect the corrosion and tightening of the ICU ground terminals, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the ground circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ICU replacement.

6. CAN serial data circuit inspection

Inspect the connection status of the CAN circuit junction connector between the ICU and the DLC, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the CAN circuit between the ICU and the DLC or a short circuit between CAN circuits, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ICU replacement.

7. ICU replacement

Replace the ICU. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) ICU （Immobilizer with Keyless Entry） removal".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) ICU （Immobilizer with Keyless Entry） installation".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) ICU （Immobilizer with Keyless Entry） setting".

8. Repair verification 

Reconnect all of the fuses and harness connectors that have been disconnected. 

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Verify if communication with the ICU is available with a scan tool.

If communications cannot be established, go to ICU harness connector inspection.
