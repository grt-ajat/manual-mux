Curtain airbag scrapping (All models)

1. Undeployed curtain airbag safety information

Caution

  • Take extra care when handling and storing an undeployed airbag.
  • If the airbag deploys, material on the surface of the airbag may be thrown outwards with great force due to the sudden generation of gas.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the bag opening is facing away from your body.
  • This is in order to minimize injury in case the airbag deploys.
  • Never carry the airbag assembly by holding the wiring or connector.

Note

  • Procedure for transporting an undeployed airbag

Caution

  • Service personnel should refer to the latest Service Bulletins for proper airbag transport procedures.

Note

  • Precautions regarding the airbag assembly scrapping procedure

Caution

  • During the course of a vehicle's service life, certain situations may arise which will necessitate the disposal of an undeployed airbag.
  • This information covers the proper procedures for disposing of an undeployed airbag.
  • An undeployed airbag must not be disposed of through normal refuse channels.
  • Before an airbag can be disposed of, it must be deployed.
  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure can result in injury due to airbag deployment.
  • The airbag may be deployed either inside or outside the vehicle.
  • The airbag may be deployed either inside or outside the vehicle.
  • Failure to dispose of in accordance with the proper disposal procedure may be a violation of federal, state, or local laws.

2. Deployed curtain airbag safety information

Caution

  • Be sure to wear gloves and safety glasses when handling an airbag after deployment.
  • When handling the airbag, the safety precautions must be observed.
  • The surface of the deployed airbag may contain a small amount of sodium hydroxide.
  • This is a byproduct of the deployment reaction, and may cause irritation if it comes in contact with the skin or eyes.
  • Wash hands with soap after handling.

3. Curtain airbag (deployment outside vehicle) (method of securing to tire) scrapping

Caution

  • When deploying an undeployed airbag outside the vehicle, the deployment procedure must be strictly followed.
  • Continue to wear safety glasses until the deployed airbag is disposed of.
  • Before performing the disposal procedure, you should be fully familiar with SRS repairs and with proper handling of the airbag.
  • Go over the procedure carefully before beginning work.
  • Use SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0 for this procedure.
  • The procedure must not be performed when SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0 are not available.

Warning

  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure can result in injury.
  • Never connect the SRS deployment harness to any power source before connecting to the curtain airbag.
  • Keep the deployment harness shorted until the moment when the airbag is to be deployed.
  • The airbag will deploy immediately when connected to a power source.

Caution

  • The following is the curtain airbag deployment procedure.

1. Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Note

  • Remove the key.
  • Wear safety glasses.

2. Disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery.

3. Remove the curtain airbag from vehicle.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the bag opening is facing away from your body.

4. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

Note

  • Inspect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0 for damage.
  • If the harness is damaged, discard it and obtain a new one.


SST: 1-8528-1002-0 - SRS deployment harness


SST: 5-8840-3142-0 - SRS deployment harness

Note

  • Short the 2 SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Caution

  • The SRS deployment harness should remain shorted, and absolutely must not be connected to a power source until the moment when the airbag is to be deployed.

Warning

  • Connecting the deployment harness to the power source should always be the last step in the airbag deployment procedure.
  • If this procedure is not followed correctly, there is a risk of injury.

Note

  • Connect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0.

Note

  • Clear a space on the ground about 2 m (6.6 feet) in diameter where the airbag is to be deployed.
  • An outdoor location on a paved road where people do not pass is preferred.
  • If an outdoor location is not available, select a space on the shop floor where people do not pass and where there is sufficient ventilation.
  • Ensure that no loose or flammable objects are within the deployment area.

5. Prepare the tire.

Note

  • Prepare 3 tires (15 inch or larger) without wheels, and 2 tires of the same size with wheels.

6. Connect the SRS deployment harness to the curtain airbag.

Caution

  • Verify that the SRS deployment harness is securely installed to the airbag connector.
  • Keep the deployment harness shorted until the moment when the airbag is to be deployed.
  • The airbag will deploy immediately when connected to a power source.

7. Fasten the curtain airbag to the tire.

Note

  • In 2 locations, wind the automotive wire harness (with a cross-section of 1.25 mm2 or more) or fix with wire.
  • Do not wrap around the deployment surface.

Note

  • Pile up the tires as shown in the illustration.
  • Secure firmly using ropes at 4 locations to prevent the tires from tumbling down.

  1. Tire for securing airbag
  2. Tire without wheel
  3. Tire with wheel

Note

  • Stretch the SRS deployment harness from the airbag to its maximum length.
  • Place a power source near the shorted end of the SRS deployment harness.
  • A vehicle battery of minimum 12 V and minimum 2 A is recommended as the power source.

Caution

  • Confirm that no people or loose or flammable objects are present in the area around the airbag.
  • Notify all persons in the nearby area that an airbag will be deployed.
  • The deployment will be accompanied by a very loud noise which may startle persons who are not expecting it.

Note

  • Separate the 2 banana plugs on the SRS deployment harness.

Caution

  • When the airbag deploys, the gas inside the airbag expands violently.

Warning

  • The airbag will immediately deploy when connected to a power source.

8. Connect the SRS deployment harness to power source.

Caution

  • Again notify all persons in the nearby area that an airbag will be deployed.

Note

  • Connect the SRS deployment harness to the power source to immediately deploy the airbag.

9. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from power source.

Note

  • Short the 2 SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Note

  • In the event that the airbag is not deployed after following this procedure, immediately proceed to step 11.

Caution

  • When handling the deployed airbag, wear a pair of work gloves and safety goggles to protect your hands and eyes from possible irritation and heat.

Warning

  • When handling the deployed airbag, the safety precautions must be observed.
  • The metal parts of the airbag after deployment will be extremely hot.
  • Do not touch the metal parts of the airbag for about 30 minutes after deployment.
  • Wait for the inflator module to cool.
  • Do not place the deployed airbag close to any flammable object.
  • If this procedure is not followed correctly, there is a risk of injury.
  • If the deployed airbag must be moved before it has cool cooled, be sure to wear gloves and avoid touching any metal surfaces.

10. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the curtain airbag.

Note

  • Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the airbag as soon as possible after deployment in order to avoid damaging the SRS deployment harness.
  • Inspect the SRS deployment harness for damage, and replace if necessary.
  • Dispose of the deployed airbag through normal refuse channels.

  1. Sturdy vinyl bag
  2. Deployed airbag

Note

  • Wash hands with soap after handling.

Caution

  • The following is the procedure to be taken in case the airbag is not deployed during the above procedure.

11. Disconnect the SRS deployment harness from the curtain airbag.

Warning

  • When carrying an undeployed airbag, make sure that the bag opening is facing away from your body.

12. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

4. Curtain airbag (deployment inside vehicle) scrapping

Note

  • Deploying inside the vehicle is effective when the vehicle is being scrapped or when recovering parts that can be reused.
  • This includes, but is not limited to, the following situations.
1The vehicle has completed its useful life.
2The vehicle has been damaged beyond repair in a nondeployment type accident.
3The vehicle has been stripped or damaged beyond repair in a theft.
4Collect reusable components.

Warning

  • Never use SRS components from one vehicle in another vehicle.

Caution

  • When deploying an undeployed airbag inside the vehicle, the deployment procedure must be strictly followed.
  • Continue to wear safety glasses until the deployed airbag is disposed of.
  • Before performing the disposal procedure, you should be fully familiar with SRS repairs and with proper handling of the airbag.
  • Go over the procedure carefully before beginning work.

  • The airbag will deploy immediately when connected to a power source.

Caution

  • The following is the curtain airbag deployment procedure.

1. Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Note

  • Remove the key.
  • Remove all loose objects from inside the vehicle.
  • To reduce the risk of personal injury from the vehicle glass or the interior pieces, cover the windshield area, the rear window area, and the door window opening completely with a drop cloth, blanket, or similar item.
  • Wear safety glasses.

2. Disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery.

3. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

Note

  • Inspect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0 for damage.
