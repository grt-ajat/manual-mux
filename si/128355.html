Shift lock control circuit system check (TB-50LS)

1. Shift lock control circuit system check description of function

The automatic transmission has the built-in shift lock mechanism, and when the selector lever is placed in the P position, the shift lock solenoid is activated, preventing the shift operation from the P position. The operation from the P position becomes available when the shift lock solenoid is energized by tuning the ignition switch on and depressing the brake pedal, and the push button on the selector lever is pressed.

2. Shift lock control circuit system check

1. Checking DTCs

Check for an ABS DTC using a scan tool.

If DTC C0161 has been detected, perform diagnosis for the DTC.

If DTC C0161 has not been detected, go to Shift lock solenoid inspection.

2. Shift lock solenoid inspection

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the shift lock solenoid assembly harness connector from the interlock controller assembly.

Measure the resistance between the shift lock solenoid terminals.

Standard： 127 Ω

If it is normal, go to BACK LIGHT power supply circuit inspection.

If it is outside the specified range, replace the shift lock solenoid assembly. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "5.Transmission, Transaxle 5B.Automatic(TB-50LS) selector lever assembly disassembly".

Refer to "5.Transmission, Transaxle 5B.Automatic(TB-50LS) selector lever assembly reassembly".

3. Inspection for open circuit in BACK LIGHT power supply circuit

Inspect the BACK LIGHT 15 A fuse, and repair as necessary. If the fuse blows out again, repair the short to ground in the circuit relating to the BACK LIGHT 15 A fuse. Go to Repair verification.

Disconnect the interlock controller assembly harness connector from the selector lever assembly.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Measure the voltage between the BACK LIGHT power supply circuit of the interlock controller assembly harness connector and the body ground.

Standard： 1 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in ground circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, repair an open circuit or high resistance in the BACK LIGHT power supply circuit between the BACK LIGHT 15 A fuse and the interlock controller assembly. Go to Repair verification.

4. Inspection for open circuit in ground circuit

Measure the voltage between the BACK LIGHT power supply circuit of the interlock controller assembly harness connector and the ground circuit.

Standard： 1 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in stoplight switch signal circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, repair an open circuit or high resistance in the ground circuit between the interlock controller assembly and the frame ground. Go to Repair verification.

5. Inspection for open circuit in stoplight switch signal circuit

Depress the brake pedal and measure the voltage between the stoplight switch signal circuit of the interlock controller assembly harness connector and the body ground.

Standard： 1 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection of interlock controller assembly harness connector.

If the reading is less than the specified value, repair an open circuit or high resistance in the stoplight switch signal circuit between the stoplight switch and the interlock controller assembly. Go to Repair verification.

6. Inspection of interlock controller assembly harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the interlock controller assembly harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If it is normal, replace the interlock controller assembly. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "5.Transmission, Transaxle 5B.Automatic(TB-50LS) selector lever assembly disassembly".

Refer to "5.Transmission, Transaxle 5B.Automatic(TB-50LS) selector lever assembly reassembly".

7. Repair verification

Reconnect all of the fuses and harness connectors that have been disconnected.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Depress the brake pedal.

Confirm that the selector lever can be operated from the P position.

If it cannot be operated, go to Checking DTCs.
