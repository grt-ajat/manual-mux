DTC P0651 (Flash Code 56) Sensor Reference Voltage 2 Circuit (4JJ1)

1. DTC P0651 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Circuit diagnosis

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the accelerator pedal position sensor harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Measure the voltage between the 5 V reference circuit of accelerator pedal position sensor 2 and the body ground.

Standard： 4.7 to 5.3 V

If the reading is within the specified range, replace the accelerator pedal assembly. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1I.Speed Control Systems(4JJ1) accelerator pedal assembly removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1I.Speed Control Systems(4JJ1) accelerator pedal assembly installation".

If the reading is more than the specified value, go to Inspection 1 for short circuit in 5 V reference circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection of barometric pressure sensor.

3. Inspection of barometric pressure sensor

Connect the DMM to the accelerator pedal position sensor harness connector.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the barometric pressure sensor harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Observe the voltage display on the DMM.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, replace the barometric pressure sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) barometric pressure sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) barometric pressure sensor installation".

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection of fuel rail pressure sensor.

4. Inspection of fuel rail pressure sensor

Connect the DMM to the accelerator pedal position sensor harness connector.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the fuel rail pressure sensor harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Observe the voltage display on the DMM.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, replace the fuel rail pressure sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JJ1) FRP sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1C.Fuel System(4JJ1) FRP sensor installation".

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection of CMP sensor.

5. Inspection of CMP sensor

Connect the DMM to the accelerator pedal position sensor harness connector.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the CMP sensor harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Observe the voltage display on the DMM.

Standard： 4.7 V

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, replace the CMP sensor. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) CMP sensor removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) CMP sensor installation".

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection 2 for short circuit in 5 V reference circuit.

6. Inspection 1 for short circuit in 5 V reference circuit

Inspect for a short to the power supply circuit in 5 V reference circuit 2 between the ECM and accelerator pedal position sensor 2 or the barometric pressure sensor, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect 5 V reference circuit 5 between the ECM and the fuel rail pressure sensor or the CMP sensor for a short to the power supply circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

7. Inspection 2 for short circuit in 5 V reference circuit

Inspect for a short to ground or a short to the low reference circuit in 5 V reference circuit 2 between the ECM and accelerator pedal position sensor 2 or the barometric pressure sensor, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

Inspect for a short to ground or a short to the low reference circuit in 5 V reference circuit 5 between the ECM and the fuel rail pressure sensor or the CMP sensor, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

8. ECM replacement

Replace the ECM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM setting".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM writing".

2. DTC P0651 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Operate the vehicle within the conditions for setting the DTC or within the parameters observed in the Freeze Frame.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
