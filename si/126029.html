Intercooler inspection (4JJ1)

1. Intercooler inspection

1. Inspect the intercooler.

Note

  • Repair the intercooler fins if they are deformed as it can lead to a drop in heat dissipation and reduce engine performance.
  • When repairing, take sufficient care not to damage the base of the fins.

2. Intercooler cleaning

Caution

  • Do not apply high-pressure washing to the radiator, intercooler, and peripheral parts while they are installed in the vehicle.

Note

  • Before cleaning, seal the hose connections with caps, fabric tape, etc.

1. Clean the intercooler using tap water.

Note

  • Wet the front and rear surfaces of the intercooler sufficiently.
  • For fouling that can be removed by splashing tap water, thoroughly wash them off.

Caution

  • Do not use a high-pressure washing machine or brush.

2. Apply detergent to the intercooler.

Note

  • Apply a type of mild detergent which contains approximately 8 % of surface acting agent, and leave it for 10 minutes.

Caution

  • Do not use materials other than mild detergent because doing so may cause corrosion.

3. Clean the intercooler using the high-pressure washing machine.

Note

  • Wash off mild detergent with a high-pressure washing machine.

Caution

  • Apply the high pressure water perpendicular to the fins from the engine side of the intercooler.
  • Do not bring the high-pressure water too close to the fins.
  • Watch for deformation of the fins.

Note

  • If dirt is not thoroughly washed off, repeat the cleaning work.
  • If dirt remains, fine black particles are seen attached on the fins.

4. Clean the intercooler using tap water.

Caution

  • Thoroughly rinse off the detergent ingredients to prevent it from remaining on the fins and the resin portions.

5. Clean the connection using tap water.

Note

  • Remove the seal, and clean the hose connection.
  • Thoroughly clean and dry the hose connection.
