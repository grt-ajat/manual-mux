Seat belt with pretensioner scrapping (All models)

1. Undeployed seat belt with pretensioner safety information

Caution

  • Take extra care when handling and storing unopened seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • In the unlikely event that a seat belt with pretensioner or a lap pretensioner has deployed, the sudden expansion of gas may cause items in the vicinity of the seat belt with pretensioner to be blown about.

Note

  • Seat belt with pretensioner

Note

  • Seat belt with pretensioner and lap pretensioner

Warning

  • When transporting undeployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners, make sure to face the gas generator section away from your body.
  • This is to minimize the risk of injury in the event that a seat belt with pretensioner and lap pretensioner accidentally deploys.
  • Never attempt to touch or move the wiring or connectors of seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.

Note

  • Transport procedures for undeployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners

Caution

  • Service personnel should check the latest service bulletins for the appropriate seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner transporting procedures.

2. Deployed seat belt with pretensioner safety information

Caution

  • Be sure to wear gloves and safety glasses when handling seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners after activation.
  • Safety precautions must be observed when handling seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • Activated seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners may contain a small amount of sodium hydroxide.
  • This is a byproduct of the deployment reaction, and may cause irritation if it comes in contact with the skin or eyes.
  • Wash hands with soap after handling.

3. Seat belt with pretensioner (deployment procedure for disposal) safety information

Caution

  • Failure to follow correct procedures may lead to a serious accident due to malfunction of seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners during work.
  • Never disassemble seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners, because they are designed to deploy using backup power in the event of a collision, even when the battery power is cut off.
  • Do not subject seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners to impact or place them near a magnet.
  • This may cause the pretensioner to malfunction.
  • Place seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners so that the gas generator section faces outward.
  • Do not place any items on seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • In order to prevent malfunctions caused by static electricity, touch a metal part of the vehicle before beginning the procedure.
  • Perform the procedure in a well-ventilated area.
  • When deploying indoors, do not deploy near a fire alarm, smoke detector, or under a fluorescent light.
  • Take care not to inhale the smoke emitted on deployment.
  • Do not allow water or other fluids to come in contact with seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • Do not reuse removed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners in another vehicle.

4. Seat belt with pretensioner (scrapping procedure) safety information

Caution

  • During the course of a vehicle's service life, certain situations may arise which will necessitate the disposal of an unactivated seat belt with pretensioner.
  • This information covers the proper procedures for disposing of an unactivated seat belt with pretensioner.
  • An unactivated seat belt with pretensioner must not be disposed of through normal refuse channels.
  • Before a seat belt with pretensioner can be disposed of, it must be activated.
  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure can result in injury due to activation of the seat belt with pretensioner.
  • The seat belt with pretensioner may be activated either inside or outside the vehicle.
  • Failure to dispose of in accordance with the proper disposal procedure may be a violation of federal, state, or local laws.

5. Undeployed seat belt with pretensioner (deployment outside vehicle) scrapping

Caution

  • When activating an unactivated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner outside the vehicle, the deployment procedure must be strictly followed.
  • Continue to wear safety glasses until the activated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner is disposed of.
  • Before performing the disposal procedure, you should be fully familiar with SRS repairs and with proper handling of the seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner.
  • Go over the procedure carefully before beginning work.
  • Use SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0, 5-8840-2881-1 and 5-8840-3142-0, as well as SRS deployment fixture 5-8840-2420-0 for this procedure.
  • The procedure must not be performed when SRS deployment harness 1-8528-1002-0,5-8840-2881-1, 5-8840-3142-0, and SRS deployment fixture 5-8840-2420-0 are not available.

Warning

  • Failure to follow the proper disposal procedure can result in injury.
  • Never connect the SRS deployment harness to any power source before connecting it to the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner.
  • Keep the deployment harness shorted until the moment when the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner is to be activated.
  • The seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner will immediately activate when a power source is connected to it.

Caution

  • The following is the procedure for activating the seat belt with pretensioner and the lap pretensioner.

1. Turn OFF the ignition switch.

3. Remove the seat belt with pretensioner from vehicle.

4. Inspect the SRS deployment harness.

Note

  • Inspect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0,5-8840-2881-1 and 5-8840-3142-0 for damage.

Note

  • Short the 2 SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Caution

  • The SRS deployment harness should remain shorted, and absolutely must not be connected to a power source until the moment when the seat belt with pretensioner is to be activated.

Warning

  • Connecting the deployment harness to the power source should always be the last step in the seat belt with pretensioner activation procedure.
  • If this procedure is not followed correctly, there is a risk of injury.

Note

  • When you are deploying a seat belt with pretensioner, connect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-2881-1.
  • When you are deploying a lap pretensioner, connect SRS deployment harnesses 1-8528-1002-0 and 5-8840-3142-0.

Note

  • Clear a space on the ground about 2 m (6.6 feet) in diameter where the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner is to be activated.
  • An outdoor location on a paved road where people do not pass is preferred.
  • If an outdoor location is not available, select a space on the shop floor where people do not pass and where there is sufficient ventilation.
  • Ensure that no loose or flammable objects are within the deployment area.

Note

  • Short the 2 SRS deployment harnesses together by securely inserting one banana plug into the other.

Note

  • If the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner does not activate after following this procedure, immediately proceed to step 11.

Caution

  • When handling the activated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner, wear a pair of work gloves and safety goggles to protect your hands and eyes from possible irritation and heat.

Warning

  • Safety precautions must be observed when handling an activated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner.
  • After activation, the metal parts of the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner will be very hot.
  • Do not touch the metal portions of the seat belt with pretensioner or the lap pretensioner for about 30 minutes after activation.
  • Wait for the gas generator to cool.
  • Do not place an activated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner near any flammable objects.
  • If this procedure is not followed correctly, there is a risk of injury.
  • If the activated seat belt with pretensioner or lap pretensioner must be moved before it has cooled, be sure to wear gloves and avoid touching any metal surfaces.

6. Undeployed seat belt with pretensioner (deployment inside vehicle) scrapping

Note

  • Deploying inside the vehicle is effective when the vehicle is being scrapped or when recovering parts that can be reused.
  • This includes, but is not limited to, the following situations.
1The vehicle has completed its useful life.
2The seat belt with pretensioner was not activated during an accident in which the vehicle suffered unrepairable damage.
3The vehicle has been stripped or damaged beyond repair in a theft.
4Collect reusable components.

Warning

  • Never use SRS components from one vehicle in another vehicle.

Caution
