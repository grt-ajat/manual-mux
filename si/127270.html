Front tongue side seat belt removal (All models)

1. Seat belt with pretensioner safety information

Note

  • Handling deployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners

Warning

  • Safety precautions must be observed when handling seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • There may be a small amount of sodium hydroxide adhering to seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners after deployment.
  • This is a byproduct of the deployment reaction, and may cause irritation if it comes in contact with the skin or eyes.
  • Be sure to wear gloves and safety glasses when handling seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners after activation.
  • Wash hands with soap after handling.

Note

  • Handling undeployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners

Warning

  • When transporting undeployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners, make sure to face the gas generator section away from your body.
  • Never carry seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners by holding the connector or harness.
  • Do not place any items on undeployed seat belts with pretensioners or lap pretensioners.
  • Not following this procedure may result in fire or injuries.

Caution

  • After the airbag is deployed, inspect for burning or melting caused by excessive heat, or any other problems.

2. SRS control unit preparation

1. Deactivate the SRS function.

Note

  • Turn the ignition switch to LOCK and remove the key.
  • Remove the SRS fuse from the fuse relay box or disconnect the battery ground cable from the battery.

Caution

  • Do not disconnect within 1 minute after turning OFF the ignition switch.
