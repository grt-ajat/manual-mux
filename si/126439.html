DTC P0568 (Flash Code 518) Cruise Control Set Signal (4JK1)

1. DTC P0568 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JK1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the cruise control switch harness connector.

Connect a fused jumper wire between the cruise control switch signal circuit and the low reference circuit.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Observe the Cruise Cancel Switch display on the scan tool.

If ON is displayed when the circuits are connected by a jumper wire and OFF is displayed when the circuits are not connected, replace the cruise control switch. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9C.Electrical Control, Entertainment(All models) cruise control steering wheel switch removal".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9C.Electrical Control, Entertainment(All models) cruise control steering wheel switch installation".

If the display does not change, go to Inspection for short to power supply in signal circuit.

3. Inspection for short to power supply in signal circuit

Inspect the cruise control switch signal circuit for a short to the power supply circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

4. ECM replacement

Replace the ECM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JK1) ECM removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JK1) ECM installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JK1) ECM setting".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JK1) ECM writing".

2. DTC P0568 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

While observing the Cruise Set/Coast Switch display on the scan tool, operate the cruise control set switch.

Verify that the Cruise Set/Coast Switch display shows ON and OFF on the scan tool.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
