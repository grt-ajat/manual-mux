DTC P0661 (Flash Code 58) Intake Manifold Tuning Valve Control Circuit Low (4JJ1)

1. DTC P0661 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "202.Description Engine 15C.Functional Inspection(4JJ1) diagnostic system check-engine controls".

2. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Disconnect the swirl control solenoid valve harness connector.

Connect a test lamp between the swirl control solenoid valve power supply circuit and the body ground.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

If the test lamp illuminates, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the test lamp does not illuminate, go to Inspection for open circuit in power supply circuit.

3. Circuit diagnosis 2

Connect a test lamp between the swirl control solenoid valve control circuit and the power supply circuit.

Perform the Swirl Control Solenoid Control with a scan tool.

Command the solenoid valve ON or OFF　while observing the test lamp.

If the test lamp turns ON and OFF with each command, go to Inspection of swirl control solenoid valve　harness connector.

If the test lamp remains ON with each command, go to Inspection for short circuit in control circuit.

If the test lamp does not illuminate with each command, go to Inspection for open circuit in control circuit.

4. Inspection for open circuit in power supply circuit

Inspect the ENGINE 10 A fuse, and repair as necessary. If the fuse blows out again, repair the short to ground in the circuit relating to the ENGINE 10 A fuse. Go to Repair verification.

Note

  • The swirl control solenoid valve shares the power supply circuit with other components. The ENGINE 10 A fuse blowout may set DTCs on components that share this circuit.

If the fuse is normal, repair an open circuit or high resistance in the power supply circuit between the ENGINE 10 A fuse and the swirl control solenoid valve. Go to Repair verification.

5. Inspection for short circuit in control circuit

Inspect for a short to ground in the swirl control solenoid valve control circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to ECM replacement.

6. Inspection for open circuit in control circuit

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the swirl control solenoid valve control circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of ECM harness connector.

7. Inspection of swirl control solenoid valve harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the swirl control solenoid valve harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, replace the swirl control solenoid valve. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) swirl control solenoid valve removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1B.Mechanical(4JJ1) swirl control solenoid valve installation".

8. Inspection of ECM harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the ECM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to ECM replacement.

9. ECM replacement

Replace the ECM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM removal".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM installation".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM setting".

Refer to "1.Engine 1J.Electrical(4JJ1) ECM writing".

2. DTC P0661 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Operate the vehicle within the conditions for setting the DTC or within the parameters observed in the Freeze Frame.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
