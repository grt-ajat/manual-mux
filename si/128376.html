DTC P0707 (Flash code 17) Transmission Range Switch Circuit Low (TB-50LS)

1. DTC P0707 description of DTC

The inhibitor switch is connected to the manual valve lever shaft, and is installed onto the transmission case. The inhibitor switch is supplied with the ignition power source, and when each contact point of P, R, N and D is closed through the selector lever operation, the ignition voltage is supplied to the TCM. The TCM detects the shift poisons based on the signals from the inhibitor switch.
The TCM sets the DTC when the voltage is low through all the shift positions.

2. DTC P0707 condition for setting the DTC

DTCs P0722, P0882, P0883, P0961, P0962, P0963, P0965, P0966, P0967, P0973, P0974, P0976, P0977, P0985, P0986, P1790, P1791, P2719, P2720, P2721, U0073 and U0100 are not set.
The battery voltage is between 10.2 - 15.5 V.
The engine speed is 550 rpm or more.
All of the following conditions are continued for 30 seconds or more.
The vehicle speed detected by the output speed sensor is 30 km/h (19 mph) or more.
The TCM detects that the signals from the inhibitor switch are all OFF.

3. DTC P0707 action taken when the DTC sets

Note

  • Euro4 model

When the diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM flashes the check trans warning light on the second consecutive driving cycle.
When the diagnostic runs and fails, the TCM requests to illuminate the MIL on the second consecutive driving cycle.
When the diagnostic reports a failure on the second consecutive driving cycle, the TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The ECM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The ECM stores this information in the Freeze Frame/Failure Records.
The ECM stores DTC P0700 in ECM history during the second consecutive driving cycle in which the diagnostic fails.
The TCM inhibits the cruise control.
The TCM inhibits the manual mode.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the reverse lock-out control.
The TCM fixes the gear shift position to the D position.

Note

  • Euro2 and Euro3 model

The TCM flashes the check trans warning light on the second consecutive driving cycle that the diagnostic runs and fails.
If the diagnostic reports a failure on the second consecutive driving cycle, the TCM stores this information in the Failure Records.
The TCM inhibits the cruise control.
The TCM inhibits the manual mode.
The TCM inhibits the slope control.
The TCM inhibits the reverse lock-out control.
The TCM fixes the gear shift position to the D position.
