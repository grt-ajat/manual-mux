DTC B2495 Tailgate Latch Unlock Circuit Malfunction (BCM)

1. DTC B2495 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "223.Description Body Control 36C.Functional Inspection(BCM with Keyless Entry) diagnostic system check-body controls".

Refer to "223.Description Body Control 36C.Functional Inspection(BCM with PESS) diagnostic system check-body controls".

2. Circuit diagnosis

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the BCM harness connector.

Connect a test lamp between the battery voltage (power 2) circuit and the body ground.

If the test lamp illuminates, go to BCM harness connector inspection.

If the test lamp does not illuminate, go to Inspection for open circuit in BCM power supply circuit.

3. Inspection for open circuit in BCM power supply circuit

Inspect the ROOM 10 A fuse, and replace as necessary. If the fuse blows out again, repair the short to ground in the circuit relating to the ROOM 10 A fuse. Go to Repair verification.

If it is normal, repair an open circuit or high resistance in the battery voltage (power 2) circuit. Go to Repair verification.

4. BCM harness connector inspection

Inspect the connection status of the BCM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to BCM replacement.

5. BCM replacement

Replace the BCM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) BCM removal".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) BCM installation".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) BCM setting".

2. DTC B2495 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Unlock using the tailgate unlock switch.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
