3. Shift solenoid A (S1) inspection

1. Measure a resistance using tester.

Note

  • Measure the resistance between the shift solenoid A (S1) terminal and shift solenoid A (S1) body.

Caution

  • Do not apply impact to the shift solenoid A (S1).
  • Do not damage the terminals.
Spec
： 11 to 15 Ωat 20 ℃ {68 °F}
Reference value
： 8.4 to 11.5 Ωat -40 ℃ {-40 °F}
： 16.6 to 22.6 Ωat 150 ℃ {302 °F}

2. Inspect air leakage using the battery.

Note

  • Check that the shift solenoid A (S1) operates when connecting the battery positive terminal to the shift solenoid A (S1) terminal and the battery negative terminal to the shift solenoid A (S1) body.

Caution

  • Do not apply impact to the shift solenoid A (S1).
  • Do not damage the terminals.
  • Do not damage the strainer on the tip of the shift solenoid A (S1) while inspect operation.
Normal open type : Shift solenoid A (S1)
No connect batteryLeak air
Connect batteryNo leak air

5. Inspect the pressure control solenoid A (SL1) using the battery.

Note

  • Check that the pressure control solenoid A (SL1) operates when connecting the battery positive terminal to the positive terminal of the pressure control solenoid A (SL1) via a bulb (12 - 21 W), and the battery negative terminal to the negative terminal.

Caution

  • Do not apply impact to the pressure control solenoid A (SL1).
  • Do not damage the terminals.
  • Do not keep on having been connected for a long time to give a pressure control solenoid A (SL1) an over current. (less than 1 min.)
