DTC P0116 (Flash Code 23) Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor Circuit Range/Performance (4JK1)

1. DTC P0116 description of DTC

The engine coolant temperature sensor is installed in the thermostat housing. The engine coolant temperature sensor is a variable resistor and measures the temperature of the engine coolant. When the ECM detects that the difference of the engine coolant temperature is smaller than the calculated range under the predetermined conditions, this DTC is set. This DTC only runs once per ignition cycle within the effective conditions.

2. DTC P0116 condition for setting the DTC

DTCs P0117, P0118, P0201 - P0204, P0500, P0501, P1261, P1262, P2146, and P2149 are not set.
The ignition switch is ON.
The engine coolant temperature is between -10 - 110°C (14 - 230°F).
The vehicle driving time is 18 minutes or more.
The engine speed is 1200 rpm or more and the engine runtime is more than 5 minutes.
The ECM detects that the difference between the maximum and minimum engine coolant temperatures is less than 5°C (9°F).

3. DTC P0116 action taken when the DTC sets

The ECM illuminates the MIL on the second driving cycle that the diagnostic runs and fails.
The ECM records the operating conditions at the time the diagnostic fails. The first time the diagnostic fails, the ECM stores this information in the Failure Records. If the diagnostic reports a failure on the second consecutive driving cycle, the ECM records the operating conditions at the time of failure and stores this information in the Freeze Frame and updates the Failure Records.
The ECM limits fuel injection quantity.
The ECM inhibits the EGR control.
The ECM inhibits the cruise control.
