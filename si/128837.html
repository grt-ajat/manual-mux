EHCU removal (All models)

1. EHCU safety information

Caution

  • When welding the vehicle using an electrical arc welder, remove the EHCU from the vehicle.
  • When removing the EHCU from the vehicle, disconnect the battery cable and connectors.
  • Installation is the reverse order of the removal procedure.
  • Do not install devices that emit powerful radio waves such as wireless devices near the EHCU.
  • Keep water away from the EHCU.
  • Never loosen the screws of the EHCU.
  • Do not paint it.
  • Do not allow metal objects such as screwdrivers or tester probes directly contact the connector pins terminals.
  • Handle with due attention to static electricity.
  • Do not directly apply external voltage to the terminals.
  • Do not hold the pipe on the top of the EHCU when handling it.
  • Never loosen the EHCU bolts and nuts.
  • Should they be loosened, do not reuse the EHCU.
  • When replacing the EHCU, it is needed to write the vehicle information to a new EHCU.
  • When replacing the EHCU, upload the data in the EHCU before replacement to a scan tool, and then remove the EHCU.
  • If communication cannot be made with a scan tool, remove it with no change.
  • Use the same scan tool from the beginning to the completion of a work for replacement. If a scan tool is replaced during a work, correct information cannot be written.

2. EHCU upload

Note

  • The following applies to the models with the ESC.
  • If the EHCU to be replaced is not able to communicate with a scan tool, do not perform this operation.

1. Connect a scan tool to the DLC.

2. Turn ON the ignition switch.

3. Select diagnosis.

4. Select the chassis.

5. Select ABS/ESC.

6. Select the programming.

7. Select Module Information.

Note

  • Record the 8 digits of ESC Data File Partnumber.

8. Select Upload Vehicle Configuration Data.

Note

  • Upload the vehicle data to a scan tool by following the on-screen instructions.

3. EHCU disconnect

1. Disconnect the harness connector from the EHCU.

4. Brake pipe disconnect

1. Disconnect the brake pipe from the EHCU.

Caution

  • After removing the brake pipe, cover the opening with a plug or tape to prevent foreign objects from entering.

Note

  • Models without ESC
  • Loosen the 5 flare nuts to disconnect the brake pipe.

Note

  • The following applies to models with ESC.
  • Loosen the 6 flare nuts to disconnect the brake pipe.
