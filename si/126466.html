Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess (4JJ1)

1. Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess description of symptom

The engine output is less than expected. The engine rpm hardly increases or does not increase at all even when the accelerator pedal is depressed.

2. Lack of power, sluggishness or sponginess diagnostics

Note

  • Preliminary inspection

Inspect the engine control system.
Compare the vehicle with a similar vehicle. Check if any problem exists in the vehicle.
Remove the air cleaner and inspect for contamination, collapsing in the air duct, or any condition that restricts the flow.
Check whether the tire size was changed.
Check if excessively heavy loads are carried.
Inspect for clutch slip.
Inspect for brake drag.
Inspect whether the transmission shift pattern and the down shift operation are normal.
Inspect the fuel quality.
Inspect the engine oil level and the oil quality.
Use the scan tool in order to make sure the Vehicle Speed display matches the value on the vehicle speedometer.
Ensure that the ground of the ECM is free of dirt and is fixed securely in the correct position.
Verify the injector ID code of each cylinder.
Check the scan tool data list.
Check for related Service Bulletins.

Note

  • Sensor inspection

Compare each display for Engine Coolant Temperature (ECT), Intake Air Temperature (IAT), and Fuel Temperature with the scan tool data list when the engine is stopped and cold. If the difference between the temperature values is 5 °C (9 °F) or more when the engine has cooled, inspect for high resistance in each circuit and for sensor failure.
{When the ignition switch is turned ON, the MAF sensor is heated, and as a result, the IAT shows a higher value than the normal intake air temperature.}
Check if an abnormality is observed in the Mass Air Flow (MAF) display on the scan tool.
Observe the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Sensor display on the scan tool. It is normal if the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) Sensor display shows 0.9 - 1.0 V when the engine is not started with the ignition switch turned ON after at least 1 minute from the stop of engine operation. If not, inspect for high resistance in each circuit and for any sensor failure.
Fully depress the accelerator pedal while idling in neutral, and verify the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) and Desired Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) displays. It is normal if the Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display and the Desired Fuel Rail Pressure (FRP) display follow each other within a difference of 5 MPa {725 psi}.
Observe the accelerator pedal position. It is normal if the Accelerator Pedal Position (APP) display changes linearly from 0 to 100% according to the accelerator pedal operation.
Verify the Boost Pressure and Barometric Pressure (BARO) displays with the ignition switch turned on and the engine not started. It is normal if the difference between both data displays is within 7.0 kPa (1.0 psi).

Note

  • Fuel system inspection

Inspect for air in the fuel system.
Inspect for water in the fuel.
Inspect for external fuel leakage and excessive engine oil.
Inspect if the fuel is waxy or freezing.
Inspect whether the fuel filter indicator light turns on continuously or frequently when the engine is running.
Inspect the fuel filter element for clogging.
Inspect for fuel leakage from the pressure limiter and the injector.
Inspect for a stuck injector nozzle.
Inspect for collapsing, twisting, improper tightening, cracking, or clogging in the fuel system between the fuel tank and the fuel supply pump.
Inspect the vent valve and the hose of the fuel tank for clogging.
{The piping may be clogged due to foreign material from the fuel tank having entered the fuel system.}
Inspect the fuel supply pump operation.
{The fuel supply pump must be timed with the engine, and the adjusted value should be written into the ECM.}
Perform the Cylinder Balance Test with a scan tool.
Perform the Injector Forced Drive with a scan tool.
Observe the Fuel Compensation display of each cylinder on the scan tool while idling.

Note

  • Air intake system inspection

Inspect the air cleaner and air intake duct for clogging, holes and leakage.
Inspect for any condition that restricts the flow within the turbocharger inlet duct.
Inspect for stuck-close intake throttle valve.
Inspect for clogging or air leakage in the intake manifold.
Inspect the MAF sensor for dirt and damage.
Perform the swirl control solenoid control with the scan tool. Inspect the diaphragm valve operation when the ON/OFF command is executed on the diaphragm valve.
Inspect the turbocharger turbine wheel, shaft and compressor wheel for worn and damage.
Inspect the operation of nozzle control actuator of the turbocharger. Refer to Turbocharger control system check.

Note

  • Exhaust system inspection

Inspect for any condition that restricts the flow of the exhaust system.

Note

  • Engine mechanical inspection

Inspect the compression pressure of the engine.
Inspect the valve clearance.
Inspect the valve spring for damage and fatigue.
Inspect the camshaft for worn.

Note

  • Other inspections

Inspect whether the EGR system is operating properly. Refer to "EGR control system check".
Observe the Neutral Switch display on the scan tool.
Inspect whether the engine has overheated.
Inspect the operation of the A/C.

Note

  • Other than automatic transmission model

Inspect whether the EGR system is operating properly. Refer to "EGR control system check".
Observe the Neutral Switch display on the scan tool.
Inspect whether the engine has overheated.
Inspect the operation of the A/C.
Inspect the operation of the torque converter clutch.
