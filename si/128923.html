Tire wear inspection (All models)

1. Tire wear inspection description of function

Illustration No.Conditions of wornMain causes and locations to inspect
1Spot worn
Worn is generated disproportionately toward the shoulders, and in extreme cases, the tire can become polygon-shaped.		The tire wheel is not a true circle or is deformed.
The hub or knuckle is not a true circle or is deformed.
Play in the hub bearing or lower link ball joint is excessive.
Rotating parts are unbalanced.
2Tread worn only on one sideRotating parts are unbalanced.
The tire wheel is not a true circle or is deformed.
The hub or knuckle is not a true circle or is deformed.
3Partial tread wornIf there is spot worn caused by sudden braking or abrupt steering, that will lead to partial tread worn.
4Shoulder worn
Normally, worn occurs on the outer shoulder.		Camber or toe-in failure.
Shoulder worn is caused by repeated sudden cornering.
5Symmetrical worn of the shoulderThe tire wheel is not a true circle or is deformed.
Play in the hub bearing or lower link ball joint is excessive.
6Shoulder wornInsufficient air pressure.
7Feather-like thread worn only on one sideWorn is caused by repeated sudden cornering.
Camber or toe-in failure.

If any abnormality is found, inspect the tire air pressure immediately. The tire air pressure significantly affects tire worn.
Next, if a problem in the wheel alignment cannot be found in the wheel alignment inspection, inspect the shock absorbers or tire balance.
