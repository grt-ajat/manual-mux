Scan tool does not communicate with CAN device (4JJ1)

1. Scan tool does not communicate with CAN device description of function

The scan tool uses the DLC to communicate with the CAN circuit that has a communication speed of 500 kbps. The twist pair wire is terminated at the two 120 Ω resistances, with one installed inside the ECM and the other inside the instrument panel cluster. The ECM, TCM, TCCM, EHCU, ICU, SRS control unit, BCM, instrument panel cluster, and PESS control unit communicate with the scan tool via the CAN communication bus. The EGR valve, DRM, and auto A/C control unit use the same CAN communication bus circuit when the vehicle is running, but do not use it for communicating with the scan tool.

2. Scan tool does not communicate with CAN device

Note

  • The following conditions cause a loss of CAN serial data communication.
  • Open circuit in the CAN serial data circuit.
  • Short circuit between the CAN Low circuit and the CAN High circuit.
  • Short to ground in the CAN serial data circuit or the power supply circuit.
  • Failure inside the module, which causes a short to ground or a short to the power supply circuit in the CAN serial data circuit.

2. Communication status verification

Caution

  • If the IDSS is in use, check for a malfunction in the IDSS interface equipment. If the IDSS interface equipment LED does not illuminate, refer to the scan tool startup failure chart and inspect the power supply.

Verify the vehicle specifications and the status of communication with the ECM, TCM, TCCM, EHCU, ICU, SRS control unit, BCM, instrument panel cluster, and PESS control unit using a scan tool.

If communication cannot be established with specific control modules, go to Inspection of scan tool communication of each control module.

If communication cannot be established with all control modules, go to Inspection of DLC.

16. Inspection for open circuit or short circuit in CAN communication circuit

Connect the PESS control unit harness connector or instrument panel cluster harness connector.

Inspect for an open circuit in the CAN serial data circuit between each module, a short circuit between the CAN Low circuit and the CAN High circuit, and a short to ground or the power supply circuit, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.
