DTC B0685 Indicator Output Malfunction (Passive Entry and Start System)

1. DTC B0685 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "229.Description Security and Locks Control 42C.Functional Inspection(Passive Entry and Start System) diagnostic system check-passive entry and start system".

2. Fuse inspection

Inspect the METER 10A fuse, and repair as necessary. If the fuse blows out again, repair the short to ground in the circuits relating to the METER 10A fuse. Go to Repair verification.

If the fuse is normal, go to Circuit diagnosis 1.

3. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the PESS control unit harness connector.

Verify that the PESS warning light illuminates.

If the PESS warning light does not illuminate, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the PESS warning light illuminates, repair the short to ground in the PESS warning light control circuit. Go to Repair verification.

4. Circuit diagnosis 2

Remove the METER 10A fuse.

Measure the voltage between the PESS control unit harness connector PESS warning light control circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 1 V

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 3.

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, repair the short to the power supply circuit of the PESS warning light control circuit. Go to Repair verification.

5. Circuit diagnosis 3

Install the METER 10A fuse.

Connect a fused jumper wire between the PESS warning light control circuit of the PESS control unit harness connector and the body ground.

Verify that the PESS warning light illuminates.

If the PESS warning light does not illuminate, go to Circuit diagnosis 4.

If the PESS warning light illuminates, go to PESS control unit harness connector inspection.

6. Circuit diagnosis 4

Remove the instrument panel cluster.

Connect a test lamp between the battery power supply circuit of the instrument panel cluster harness connector and the body ground.

If the test lamp does not illuminate, repair the open circuit or high resistance in the instrument panel cluster battery power supply circuit. Go to Repair verification.

If the test lamp illuminates, go to Inspection for open circuit in PESS warning light control circuit.

7. Inspection for open circuit in PESS warning light control circuit

Inspect the PESS warning light control circuit for an open circuit or high resistance, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Instrument panel cluster harness connector inspection.

8. PESS control unit harness connector inspection

Inspect the connection status of the PESS control unit harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, go to PESS control unit replacement.

9. Instrument panel cluster harness connector inspection

Inspect the connection status of the instrument panel cluster harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection status is normal, replace the instrument panel cluster. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) instrument panel cluster removal".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) instrument panel cluster installation".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9E.Instrumentation, Driver Info.(All models) instrument panel cluster setting".

10. PESS control unit replacement

Replace the PESS control unit. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) PESS control unit removal".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) PESS control unit installation".

Refer to "9.Body, Cab, Accessories 9J.Security and Locks(All models) PESS control unit setting".

2. DTC B0685 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Perform the Meter Indicator with a scan tool.

Command the PESS warning light ON and OFF and verify that the PESS warning light responds in accordance with the commands.

If the PESS warning light does not operate properly, go to Fuse inspection.
