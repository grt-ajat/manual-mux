DTC C0306 Motor A or B Circuit (4WD models)

1. DTC C0306 diagnostics

1. Diagnostic system check

Refer to "204.Description Driveline, Axle 17C.Functional Inspection(All models) diagnostic system check-transfer case controls".

2. Circuit diagnosis 1

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the transfer shift actuator harness connector.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Measure the voltage between the following circuits in the transfer shift actuator harness connector.
Between the drive A circuit and the body ground
Between the drive B circuit and the body ground

Standard： 4.0 V

If the voltage between the drive A circuit and the body ground is less than or the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 2.

If the voltage between the drive B circuit and the body ground is less than the specified value, go to Circuit diagnosis 3.

If both are more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection of transfer shift actuator harness connector.

3. Circuit diagnosis 2

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the TCCM harness connector.

Measure the resistance between the TCCM harness connector drive A circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 10 MΩ

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in drive circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for short circuit in drive circuit.

4. Circuit diagnosis 3

Turn OFF the ignition switch.

Disconnect the TCCM harness connector.

Measure the resistance between the TCCM harness connector drive B circuit and the body ground.

Standard： 10 MΩ

If the reading is more than or equal to the specified value, go to Inspection for open circuit in drive circuit.

If the reading is less than the specified value, go to Inspection for short circuit in drive circuit.

5. Inspection for open circuit in drive circuit

Inspect for an open circuit or high resistance in the drive A circuit or drive B circuit where a problem is found, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of TCCM harness connector.

6. Inspection for short circuit in drive circuit

Inspect for a short to ground in the drive A circuit or drive B circuit where a problem is found, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the circuit is normal, go to Inspection of TCCM harness connector.

7. Inspection of transfer shift actuator harness connector

Inspect the connection condition of the transfer shift actuator harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection condition is normal, go to Replacement of transfer shift actuator.

8. Replacement of transfer shift actuator

Replace the transfer shift actuator. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) transfer assembly disassembly".

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) transfer assembly reassembly".

9. Inspection of TCCM harness connector

Inspect the connection status of the TCCM harness connector, and repair as necessary. Go to Repair verification.

If the connection condition is normal, go to Replacement of TCCM.

10. Replacement of TCCM

Replace the TCCM. Go to Repair verification.

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) TCCM removal".

Refer to "3.Driveline, Axle 3D.Transfer Case(All models) TCCM installation".

2. DTC C0306 repair verification

Clear the DTC with a scan tool.

Turn OFF the ignition switch for at least 30 seconds.

Turn ON the ignition switch.

Drive the vehicle in 2H, 4H, and 4L modes respectively.

Observe the DTC information with a scan tool.
